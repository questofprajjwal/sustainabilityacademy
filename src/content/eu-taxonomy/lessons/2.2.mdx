## Completing the Picture

The Environmental Delegated Act was published in November 2023 and applies from January 2024. It adds technical screening criteria for the four remaining environmental objectives:

- Sustainable use and protection of water and marine resources
- Transition to a circular economy
- Pollution prevention and control
- Protection and restoration of biodiversity and ecosystems

It also amended the Climate Delegated Act to add new activities to objectives 1 and 2.

## What It Covers

The Environmental Delegated Act brings in activities that weren't addressed by the Climate Delegated Act, including:

| Objective | Example Activities |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Water | Water supply systems, nature-based water treatment, desalination, flood risk management |
| Circular economy | Repair and refurbishment, remanufacturing, marketplace platforms for reuse, material recovery |
| Pollution | Remediation of contaminated sites, pollution monitoring, manufacturing of emission reduction equipment |
| Biodiversity | Ecosystem restoration, conservation, sustainable agriculture, urban green infrastructure |

## How It Changed the Landscape

Before this act, companies could only report taxonomy eligibility and alignment against climate objectives. Many activities in sectors like waste management, water utilities, and nature-based solutions had no criteria to assess against.

With the Environmental Delegated Act:
- Companies in water, waste, and environmental services can now demonstrate alignment
- Manufacturing companies can show alignment through circular economy contributions (not just climate)
- The scope of eligible activities expanded significantly

<HighlightBox>
The Environmental Delegated Act is newer and less tested than the Climate Delegated Act. Companies reporting against objectives 3-6 for the first time may find less guidance, fewer precedents, and more interpretation questions. This will improve as reporting practice matures.
</HighlightBox>
