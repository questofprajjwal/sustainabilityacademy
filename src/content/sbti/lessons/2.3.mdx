Having established what must be covered in the target boundary and how it must be measured, the SBTi criteria turn to two further questions: over what timeframe must the target be set, and how much reduction must it commit to? These are the criteria that directly determine whether a target deserves to be called "science-based." A company could have a perfectly constructed inventory and still fail validation if its target horizon is too short or its ambition level is calibrated to a 3C warming pathway rather than 1.5C.

Criteria C13 through C21 cover the timeframe rules (C13-C14), scope 1 and 2 ambition requirements (C15-C17), scope 3 ambition requirements (C18-C19), combined target structures (C20), and renewable electricity targets (C21). Recommendations R5 through R8 provide complementary guidance on each of these dimensions.

## Timeframe: The 5-to-10 Year Window (C13)

**C13** establishes the permitted timeframe for near-term targets. Absolute and intensity-based emission reduction near-term targets must cover a minimum of 5 years and a maximum of 10 years from the date the target is submitted to the SBTi for validation. The choice of base year must be no earlier than 2015. Scope 1 and scope 2 targets must use the same base year.

<HighlightBox>
C13 key rules:
- Minimum target horizon: 5 years from submission date.
- Maximum target horizon: 10 years from submission date.
- Base year: no earlier than 2015.
- S1 and S2 targets must share the same base year.
- The SBTi does not accept multi-year average base years (unless specified in sector guidance).
</HighlightBox>

For submissions in 2025, the most recent inventory (required for C14) must be no earlier than 2023 -- meaning allowable most recent years are 2023 and 2024. Companies may not apply base year emissions to the most recent year using proxy data.

Scope 3 targets are recommended (R6) but not required to use the same base year as scope 1 and 2 targets. However, base years across different scope 3 targets must be the same.

**R5 -- Long-term targets:** Targets that cover more than 10 years from the date of submission are considered long-term targets and can only be validated in accordance with the SBTi Corporate Net-Zero Standard Criteria. Near-term criteria (C13-C21) do not apply to long-term targets.

**R6 -- Consistency:** The SBTi recommends that companies use the same base years for all near-term targets (including scope 3 where possible), to simplify reporting and avoid confusion.

**R7 -- 2030 target year (new in V5.3):** It is recommended that companies select a 2030 target year for all near-term targets. Applying this recommendation exempts a company from the 5-to-10 year timeframe requirement in C13. This new recommendation was introduced to support alignment with the mandatory transition period of the forthcoming SBTi Corporate Net-Zero Standard V2.0 and allows companies submitting in later years to still use 2030 as their target year even if it falls within the 5-year minimum window.

<AnalogyBox>
The 5-to-10 year window is like a mortgage with a set term. Too short a term (under 5 years) means the commitment is trivially achievable and does not represent genuine medium-term planning. Too long a term (over 10 years) means the target has crossed into long-term territory and must be assessed under the more demanding Net-Zero Standard rather than the near-term criteria. The 2030 recommendation under R7 is like a government-endorsed standard mortgage term that all lenders should offer.
</AnalogyBox>

## Progress to Date: No Coasting on Past Reductions (C14)

**C14** addresses a potential loophole: a company that has already made significant emission reductions could set a future target that is effectively on a flat or slow trajectory because it already reduced emissions in previous years. C14 closes this by requiring that the minimum forward-looking ambition of near-term targets covering scope 1 and/or scope 2 emissions must be consistent with reaching net-zero by 2050 at the latest, assuming a linear absolute reduction, linear intensity reduction, or intensity convergence between the most recent year and 2050 (not increasing absolute emissions or intensity).

The most recent year used for scope 1 and 2 emissions must be the same. The most recent year used for scope 3 emissions is recommended to be the same as scope 1 and 2. This effectively means the forward-looking trajectory from today's actual emissions must be steep enough to reach net-zero by 2050 -- the company cannot coast if its historical reductions have already done some of the work.

## Scope 1 and 2 Ambition: 1.5C Minimum (C15)

**C15** sets the minimum ambition level for scope 1 and 2 near-term targets: they shall be consistent with the level of decarbonization required to keep global temperature increase to 1.5C compared to pre-industrial temperatures.

This is the headline standard that distinguishes SBTi targets from weaker, self-defined corporate climate commitments. The 1.5C alignment requirement is not a recommendation -- it is the floor below which no near-term S1 and S2 target will be validated.

**R8 -- Choosing an approach:** The SBTi recommends using the most ambitious decarbonization scenarios that lead to the earliest reductions and the least cumulative emissions.

## Absolute Scope 1 and 2 Targets (C16)

**C16** specifies the standard for absolute reduction targets. Absolute reduction targets for scope 1 and scope 2 are eligible when they are at least as ambitious as the minimum of the approved range of emissions scenarios consistent with the 1.5C goal.

In practice, this means companies using the cross-sector absolute reduction method must apply at minimum the Linear Annual Reduction (LAR) rate derived from the most conservative 1.5C-consistent scenario in the SBTi's approved scenario set. The required LAR for absolute targets is commonly cited as 4.2% per year -- this figure comes from the minimum rate needed across approved 1.5C scenarios for the cross-sector pathway.

## Intensity Scope 1 and 2 Targets (C17)

Not all companies can set absolute reduction targets -- some expect legitimate business growth that would increase absolute emissions even while becoming more carbon-efficient per unit of output. **C17** permits intensity targets for scope 1 and 2, but only under a specific condition: intensity targets are only eligible when they are modeled using an approved 1.5C sector pathway applicable to the company's business activities.

This means companies cannot freely choose to use intensity targets. They can only use an intensity approach if the SBTi has an approved sector-specific intensity pathway that applies to their sector. Without an approved sector pathway, a company must use an absolute target approach.

<ExampleBox>
**Example -- C17 eligibility:**

A steel company operates in a sector where the SBTi has an approved sector-specific intensity pathway (tonnes CO2e per tonne of steel produced). It can set an intensity target: "Reduce the CO2e intensity of steel production by 55% per tonne of crude steel by 2030 from a 2020 base year," provided this target is at least as ambitious as the approved 1.5C sector pathway for steel.

A professional services firm -- an office-based consultancy -- does not have a sector-specific 1.5C intensity pathway available. It cannot use an intensity target for scope 1 and 2. It must use an absolute reduction target per C16.
</ExampleBox>

## Scope 3 Ambition: Well-Below 2C Minimum (C18)

**C18** sets the minimum ambition level for scope 3 near-term emission reduction targets. Near-term scope 3 targets (covering total required scope 3 emissions or individual scope 3 categories) shall be aligned with methods consistent with the level of decarbonization required to keep global temperature increase well-below 2C compared to pre-industrial temperatures.

Note the deliberate difference from C15: scope 1 and 2 targets must align with 1.5C, while scope 3 targets need only align with well-below 2C. This reflects the practical reality that scope 3 emissions are harder to control directly, involve third parties across the value chain, and are measured with greater uncertainty.

<HighlightBox>
Key ambition asymmetry: S1+2 targets must be 1.5C aligned (C15). S3 emission reduction targets must be at minimum well-below 2C aligned (C18). Supplier and customer engagement targets have their own requirements under C19. This is not a loophole -- it reflects different degrees of direct control.
</HighlightBox>

## Supplier and Customer Engagement Targets (C19)

**C19** -- updated in V5.3 -- governs a specific type of scope 3 target that many companies rely on to meet their 67% coverage requirement under C6: the supplier or customer engagement target. This target type does not commit to a specific quantity of emission reductions from the company's own inventory. Instead, it commits the company to driving the adoption of science-based targets by the companies in its upstream supply chain or downstream customer base.

C19 engagement targets must meet four requirements:

**Boundary:** Companies may set engagement targets across upstream or downstream scope 3 categories.

**Formulation:** Companies must provide information in the target language on what percentage of emissions from relevant upstream and/or downstream categories is covered by the engagement target, or -- if that emissions coverage information is not available -- what percentage of annual procurement spend is covered by the target.

**Timeframe:** Engagement targets shall be fulfilled within a maximum of 5 years from the date the company's target is submitted to the SBTi for validation.

**Ambition level:** The company's suppliers and/or customers shall have science-based emission reduction targets in line with the latest version of the SBTi Corporate Near-Term Criteria.

<HighlightBox>
C19 five-year window: Unlike C13, which allows up to 10 years for regular near-term targets, engagement targets under C19 must be fulfilled within 5 years of the submission date -- not 10 years. A company submitting in 2025 with a 2035 main target year still needs its supplier engagement target fulfilled by 2030.
</HighlightBox>

**R9 -- Supplier engagement:** Companies should recommend that their suppliers use the SBTi guidance and tools available to set science-based targets. SBTi validation of supplier science-based targets is recommended but not required. Additionally, it is recommended to use 2030 or earlier as the supplier engagement target year.

<ExampleBox>
**Example -- Formulating a compliant C19 engagement target:**

Correct target language (emissions coverage): "70% of our upstream scope 3 emissions from purchased goods and services will be covered by suppliers with SBTi-validated science-based targets by 2030."

Correct target language (spend coverage, where emissions data unavailable): "65% of our annual procurement spend will be covered by suppliers with SBTi-validated science-based targets by 2029."

Incorrect (too vague): "We will engage with our key suppliers on climate."

Incorrect (wrong ambition standard): "Suppliers representing 60% of spend will have any GHG target in place by 2030." -- This does not require SBTi-aligned near-term targets, only any GHG target, which does not meet C19's ambition requirement.
</ExampleBox>

## Combined Targets (C20)

**C20** permits companies to set combined scope targets -- for example, a single target covering scope 1+2 together, or scope 1+2+3 together -- provided the SBTi can review the ambition of the individual target components and confirm each meets the relevant ambition criteria. This means a combined S1+2+3 target must have its S1+2 component verified as 1.5C aligned and its S3 component verified as well-below 2C aligned.

For sectors where minimum target ambition is further specified for scope 3 activities (as in sector-specific guidance), C24 supersedes C20. When submitting combined near-term targets, the scope 1+2 portion must be in line with at least a 1.5C scenario and the scope 3 portion must be in line with at least a well-below 2C scenario.

## Renewable Electricity Targets (C21)

**C21** establishes renewable electricity targets as an acceptable alternative to scope 2 emission reduction targets for electricity from grid procurement. Targets to actively source renewable electricity at a rate consistent with 1.5C scenarios are an acceptable alternative to scope 2 emission reduction targets over emissions from the generation of procured electricity.

The SBTi has identified the following thresholds, in line with the recommendations of RE100:
- **80% renewable electricity procurement by 2025**
- **100% renewable electricity procurement by 2030**

These are expressed as the portion of renewable electricity over total electricity use. Companies that already source electricity at or above these thresholds shall maintain or increase their use of renewable electricity to qualify. For long-term targets, companies shall maintain 100% renewable electricity procurement beyond 2030.

<HighlightBox>
C21 thresholds: 80% RE by 2025 and 100% RE by 2030. These are the minimum thresholds for using an RE target as an alternative to a scope 2 emission reduction target. Companies using location-based scope 2 accounting can still set an RE target under C21, provided they can demonstrate active sourcing of renewable electricity through market instruments.
</HighlightBox>

## Timeframe and Ambition Criteria: Summary

<ResponsiveTable>
| Criterion | Rule | Key Threshold |
|-----------|------|--------------|
| C13 | 5-10 year target horizon; base year no earlier than 2015; S1+2 share base year | 5 min / 10 max years |
| C14 | Forward trajectory must be consistent with reaching net-zero by 2050 | Net-zero by 2050 |
| C15 | S1+2 ambition: minimum 1.5C aligned | 1.5C |
| C16 | Absolute S1+2 targets: at least as ambitious as minimum approved 1.5C scenario (e.g., 4.2% LAR) | 4.2% LAR |
| C17 | Intensity S1+2 targets: only if approved 1.5C sector pathway exists | Sector pathway required |
| C18 | S3 ambition: minimum well-below 2C aligned | Well-below 2C |
| C19 | Engagement targets: boundary, formulation, 5-year window, SBTi-aligned ambition | 5-year fulfillment |
| C20 | Combined targets permitted if SBTi can verify individual scope components | Verify each component |
| C21 | RE targets: 80% by 2025, 100% by 2030 | 80% / 100% thresholds |
| R7 | Recommended 2030 target year; exempts from C13 5-10 year rule | 2030 |
</ResponsiveTable>

The timeframe and ambition criteria are where the "science-based" label is earned or denied. A target that covers the right boundary and uses the right accounting method but only commits to 20% absolute reduction over 10 years from a 2020 base year will not pass validation under C15 and C16. The science requires a faster pace of decarbonization than many companies have historically attempted, and C13 through C21 enforce that requirement precisely.
