CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project) is a not-for-profit organisation that runs a global environmental disclosure system. Its annual questionnaire is one of the most widely used tools for assessing corporate climate performance. This lesson explains how the CDP scoring methodology works, what it covers, and how it is used in peer benchmarking.

## What CDP Is and Who Uses It

CDP runs a global disclosure system through which companies provide data on their environmental impacts. Investors, purchasers, and policymakers use this data to understand how companies are managing environmental risks and opportunities.

The CDP questionnaire covers three environmental issue areas: Climate Change, Water Security, and Forests. In 2024, the previously separate questionnaires on these topics were integrated into one full corporate questionnaire. Through this questionnaire, organizations can provide data on multiple environmental issues in a single disclosure, encouraging more holistic and balanced reporting.

Separate scores continue to be awarded for each environmental issue area. This lesson focuses on the Climate Change scoring methodology, as this is the issue area most commonly used in ESG peer benchmarking engagements.

## The Four Scoring Levels

CDP evaluates responses against four criteria levels. Each level builds on the one before it. To be eligible for points at a higher level, a company must first achieve full points at the level below.

<HighlightBox>
**The four CDP scoring levels:**

**Disclosure:** The company has provided basic information. Points are awarded for completing and providing answers to questions, regardless of the content of those answers.

**Awareness:** The company demonstrates an understanding of its environmental impacts and risks. Points are awarded for demonstrating that the company has identified and understands its risks, opportunities, and dependencies.

**Management:** The company is taking action to manage its environmental impacts. Points are awarded for evidence of active management, including setting targets, implementing initiatives, and using measurement systems.

**Leadership:** The company is demonstrating best practice and taking comprehensive action. Points are awarded for evidence of ambition and leading-edge practice, such as science-based targets, robust scenario analysis, and transparent reporting aligned with financial cycles.
</HighlightBox>

Each question in the questionnaire specifies which level or levels it is scored at. Some questions carry points only at the Disclosure level; others carry points at Awareness, Management, and Leadership levels. To receive Management points for a question, a company must first achieve full Awareness points for that question. To receive Leadership points, it must first achieve full Management points.

For example, for question 1.4 (the reporting period), full Management points require that the end date falls within a recent 12-month window, demonstrating current reporting. Leadership points require that the reporting period aligns with the company's financial reporting period, which represents a higher level of integration and governance.

## How the Questionnaire Is Structured for Climate Change

The 2024 CDP full corporate questionnaire for Climate Change is organized into the following modules:

- **Module 1: Introduction** (organizational details, reporting boundary, sectors, countries of operation)
- **Module 2: Identification, assessment, and management of dependencies, impacts, risks, and opportunities** (time horizons, risk identification process, substantive impacts)
- **Module 3: Disclosure of dependencies, risks, and opportunities** (risk disclosure, opportunities disclosure)
- **Module 4: Governance** (board oversight, board competency, management responsibility, management competency, incentives, environmental policies, pension schemes, public policy engagement, communications and reporting)
- **Module 5: Business strategy** (scenario analysis, transition plans, influence of risks and opportunities on strategy and financial planning, CAPEX/OPEX alignment, low-carbon R&D, pricing environmental externalities, value chain engagement)
- **Module 6: Environmental Performance Consolidation Approach**
- **Module 7: Environmental Performance Climate Change** (emissions methodology, Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions inventory, targets, emissions reduction initiatives, product-level emissions)
- **Module 12: Environmental Performance Financial Services** (for financial sector companies)
- **Module 13: Further information and Sign off**

Modules 1 to 6 and Module 13 are integrated across climate change, water security, and forests. Modules 7 to 11 relate to environmental performance and each module is specific to an environmental issue area.

## Sector-Specific Scoring

Each question in the questionnaire is tagged to one or more sectors. Organizations in high-impact sectors are presented with questions specific to that sector and may be scored differently in general questions that display for all respondents.

The sector-based approach allows CDP to make more meaningful assessments of companies' responses, incorporating each sector's characteristics and nuances. This results in a score that reflects the company's progress in environmental stewardship and enables better benchmarking against other companies in the same sector.

The sectors used in the 2024 scoring methodology include: Agricultural commodities, Capital goods, Cement, Chemicals, Coal, Construction, Electric Utilities, Financial services, Food, beverage and tobacco, Metals and Mining, Oil and Gas, Paper and Forestry, Real Estate, Steel, Transport OEMs, Transport services, and a General category for all other sectors.

Responders are scored on their primary questionnaire sector. This means that if more than one set of sector questions applies to an organisation, not all questions will be scored. However, CDP encourages companies to respond to all questions that apply to their organisation.

<HighlightBox>
Each question is tagged to one or more of the three environmental issue areas (Climate Change, Water Security, Forests) and to one or more sectors. When using CDP data for peer benchmarking, always verify that you are comparing companies using the same sector scoring criteria, as the scoring criteria for a given question may differ between sectors.
</HighlightBox>

## How CDP Scoring Is Used in Peer Benchmarking

In a benchmarking engagement, the CDP score provides a structured view of how a company is performing relative to its peers across a common framework. Because all companies respond to the same questionnaire structure, the scores are directly comparable within the same sector.

The scoring levels provide a practical framework for gap analysis. A company that scores at the Disclosure level for a governance question has provided information but has not demonstrated active oversight. A company that scores at the Leadership level for the same question has demonstrated best practice, such as board-level climate competency and integration of climate considerations into executive incentives.

When comparing peers, a consultant can review:

- Which modules contribute most to a score gap between companies.
- Whether a company is failing to achieve Awareness points (indicating an understanding gap), Management points (indicating an action gap), or Leadership points (indicating an ambition gap).
- How sector-specific questions affect the relative standing of companies in high-impact sectors such as Oil and Gas, Electric Utilities, or Chemicals.

The point allocation for each question is set out in the methodology's scoring tables. For each question, the methodology specifies the numerator (points available) and denominator (total points in scope) for each scoring level. This allows for a precise breakdown of where each company gains or loses points relative to peers.

<AnalogyBox>
Think of the four CDP levels as a staircase. A company at the Disclosure level is standing on the ground floor: it has answered the question but shown no evidence of understanding or action. A company at the Leadership level has climbed all four flights. The scoring staircase ensures that companies cannot skip steps: you cannot earn Management points without first earning Awareness points, and you cannot earn Leadership points without first earning Management points.
</AnalogyBox>

## The Provisional Nature of the 2024 Methodology

The 2024 scoring methodology was released as a provisional document on 22 May 2024. CDP states that it expects to make future adjustments during the disclosure cycle to optimise clarity of the scoring methodology based on responses received. CDP reserves the right to amend or alter scoring criteria at any time during the disclosure cycle. Any adjustments made will be listed and displayed prominently, and companies can refer to the version control section of the document to view updates.

This means that in any benchmarking engagement using CDP 2024 scores, the consultant should verify which version of the methodology was applied when the scores were assigned.
