REGULATION (EU) 2023/1115 OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND OF THE COUNCIL
on the making available on the Union market and the export from the Union of certain commodities
and products associated with deforestation and forest degradation and repealing Regulation (EU) No 995/2010

Source: EUR-Lex (eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32023R1115)
Official Journal of the European Union, L 150, 9 June 2023
CELEX Number: 32023R1115
Adopted: 31 May 2023
Entry into force: 29 June 2023

================================================================
OVERVIEW AND SCOPE
================================================================

Regulation (EU) 2023/1115 establishes Union rules on placing and making available on the Union
market, and on the export from the Union, of certain commodities and products associated with
deforestation and forest degradation. It repeals Regulation (EU) No 995/2010 (the EU Timber
Regulation).

COVERED COMMODITIES (RELEVANT COMMODITIES):
- Cattle
- Cocoa
- Coffee
- Oil palm
- Rubber
- Soya
- Wood

COVERED PRODUCTS (RELEVANT PRODUCTS, per Annex I):
Products that contain, have been fed with, or have been made using the relevant commodities,
including but not limited to:
- Leather goods (from cattle)
- Chocolate and cocoa products
- Furniture (from wood)
- Paper and paperboard (from wood)
- Palm oil and derived products
- Soy-based products and animal feed
- Rubber and tyres
- Coffee and related products

================================================================
KEY DEFINITIONS
================================================================

DEFORESTATION: The conversion of forest to agricultural use, whether human-induced or not.

FOREST DEGRADATION: Structural changes to forest cover taking the form of the conversion of:
(a) primary forests or naturally regenerating forests into plantation forests or other wooded land; or
(b) primary forests into planted forests.

DEFORESTATION-FREE (for relevant commodities other than wood):
The relevant commodities were produced on land that has not been subject to deforestation after
31 December 2020.

DEFORESTATION-FREE (for wood):
Wood harvested from the forest without inducing forest degradation after 31 December 2020.

PRODUCED IN ACCORDANCE WITH RELEVANT LEGISLATION:
Relevant commodities and products were produced in compliance with the relevant legislation of
the country of production, including:
(a) land use rights;
(b) environmental protection;
(c) forest-related rules, including forest management and biodiversity conservation;
(d) third parties' legal rights;
(e) labour rights;
(f) human rights protected under international law;
(g) the principle of free, prior and informed consent;
(h) tax, anti-corruption, trade and customs regulations.

CUT-OFF DATE: 31 December 2020. Products produced on land subject to deforestation or
forest degradation after this date cannot be placed on the Union market or exported.

OPERATOR: Any natural or legal person who, in the course of a commercial activity, places relevant
products on the market or exports them.

TRADER: Any person in the supply chain other than the operator who makes relevant products
available on the market.

SME (Small and Medium-sized Enterprise): As defined in Commission Recommendation 2003/361/EC:
- Micro-enterprise: fewer than 10 employees, annual turnover or balance sheet not exceeding EUR 2 million
- Small enterprise: fewer than 50 employees, annual turnover or balance sheet not exceeding EUR 10 million
- Medium-sized enterprise: fewer than 250 employees, annual turnover not exceeding EUR 50 million

DUE DILIGENCE: The process of collecting information, assessing risk, and taking risk-mitigation
measures to ensure compliance with the regulation.

DUE DILIGENCE STATEMENT: An electronic declaration submitted before placing products on the
market or exporting, confirming that due diligence has been exercised and the products are
deforestation-free.

================================================================
CORE OBLIGATIONS
================================================================

ARTICLE 3 - PROHIBITION:
Relevant commodities and relevant products shall not be:
(a) placed or made available on the Union market; or
(b) exported from the Union;
unless ALL of the following conditions are satisfied:
(i) they are deforestation-free;
(ii) they have been produced in accordance with the relevant legislation of the country of production;
(iii) they are covered by a due diligence statement.

OBLIGATIONS FOR OPERATORS:

1. DUE DILIGENCE SYSTEM: Operators must establish and maintain a due diligence system comprising:

   (a) Information requirements - operators must collect and keep the following for 5 years:
       - Description, including trade name and type, of relevant commodities or products
       - Quantity (volume or weight)
       - Country of production
       - Geolocation coordinates of all plots of land where the relevant commodities were produced
       - Date or time range of production
       - Name and contact details of any supplier and any customer
       - Adequate and verifiable information that the commodities are deforestation-free

   (b) Risk assessment - operators must assess the risk of non-compliance based on:
       - Country or region classification (low, standard, or high risk)
       - Presence, quality and quantity of forests in the country or region
       - Prevalence of deforestation or forest degradation in the country
       - Reliability and accuracy of country-of-production data
       - Relevant legislation of the country of production
       - Prevalence of corruption and weaknesses in enforcement
       - Complexity of the supply chain
       - Whether there is any concern or information from third parties
       - Whether indigenous peoples' rights have been violated

   (c) Risk mitigation - when risk assessment indicates non-negligible risk:
       - Require additional information and documentation
       - Carry out independent surveys or audits
       - Take other proportionate measures

2. DUE DILIGENCE STATEMENT: Before placing products on the market or exporting, operators must
   submit a due diligence statement through the information system, confirming:
   - That due diligence has been exercised
   - That no or negligible risk was found (or, if risk found, that it has been mitigated)
   - The reference number of the due diligence statement

3. ANNUAL REPORTING (for non-SME operators): Operators that are not SMEs must publish annual
   reports on their due diligence systems, including the measures taken to comply with the regulation.

OBLIGATIONS FOR TRADERS:

Non-SME traders must exercise due diligence equivalent to operators.

SME traders must:
- Collect and keep for 5 years the due diligence statement reference numbers of their suppliers
- Collect and keep for 5 years information about the products they supply (customers, products sold)
- Be able to demonstrate traceability to the original due diligence statement

================================================================
COUNTRY RISK BENCHMARKING SYSTEM
================================================================

The Commission classifies countries (or parts of countries) into three categories:

LOW RISK:
- Countries presenting a low risk of having relevant commodities or products that do not comply
- Operators may apply simplified due diligence for products from low-risk countries
- Operators still collect required information but risk assessment is simplified

STANDARD RISK:
- Default classification for countries not designated as high or low risk
- Full due diligence obligations apply

HIGH RISK:
- Countries presenting a high risk based on deforestation and governance indicators
- Enhanced scrutiny applies
- Competent authorities increase control rates and checks

The Commission may update risk classifications based on new information, including requests from
third countries or situations where deforestation concerns emerge.

================================================================
ENFORCEMENT AND PENALTIES
================================================================

COMPETENT AUTHORITIES:
Each Member State designates one or more competent authorities responsible for:
- Checking compliance of operators and traders
- Applying the risk-based approach to checks
- Verifying due diligence statements
- Taking interim and permanent measures against non-compliant operators

COMPLIANCE CHECKS:
Competent authorities must:
- Check at least 9% of operators and products from high-risk countries
- Check at least 3% of operators and products from standard-risk countries
- Check at least 1% of operators and products from low-risk countries

INTERIM MEASURES (if non-compliance suspected):
- Suspension of placing on market (72 hours for perishables; 3 working days for others)
- Confiscation of relevant products
- Suspension of export

PENALTIES:
Member States must provide for effective, proportionate and dissuasive penalties, including:
- Fines proportional to environmental damage and value of affected commodities
- Maximum fine of at least 4% of the operator's annual turnover in the Member State
- Temporary exclusion from public procurement processes
- Temporary prohibition from placing products on the Union market

================================================================
INFORMATION SYSTEM AND TRANSPARENCY
================================================================

EU INFORMATION SYSTEM (EUDR IS):
Launched December 4, 2024. The system:
- Collects and stores due diligence statements
- Issues unique reference numbers for each statement
- Interfaces with EU customs systems
- Enables competent authorities to access and verify statements
- Supports data exchange between Member States

EU OBSERVATORY ON DEFORESTATION AND FOREST DEGRADATION:
- Monitors global deforestation and forest degradation
- Provides country-level data including geospatial information
- Supports risk classification decisions
- Informs stakeholders about deforestation trends

================================================================
IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE
================================================================

29 June 2023: Regulation enters into force

31 December 2020: Cut-off date for deforestation assessment (products must be produced
on land not deforested after this date)

30 December 2024: Obligations initially scheduled to apply (subsequently delayed)

30 December 2026: Application date for:
- Large operators (non-SMEs)
- Medium operators (non-SMEs)
- Small operators already subject to EU Timber Regulation

30 June 2027: Application date for:
- Micro-enterprises
- Small enterprises (SMEs)
- Small operators not previously under EU Timber Regulation

================================================================
INTERNATIONAL DIMENSION AND PARTNERSHIPS
================================================================

The regulation emphasizes:
- Partnerships with producer countries to support compliance without harming local economies
- Support for smallholders and indigenous and local communities
- Respect for the principle of free, prior and informed consent
- Alignment with international agreements: Paris Agreement, Convention on Biological Diversity,
  UN Sustainable Development Goals, UNDRIP
- Bilateral dialogues with major producer countries (Brazil, Indonesia, etc.)

PARTNER COUNTRIES AND COOPERATION:
- The Commission engages with producer countries to explain requirements
- Financial support available through EU development funds for capacity building
- Multi-stakeholder platform coordinates between industry, NGOs, governments, and communities

================================================================
FUTURE SCOPE EXPANSION
================================================================

The Commission must evaluate and report on extending the regulation's scope to:
- Other wooded land (within 1 year of entry into force = by June 2024)
- Other natural ecosystems including grasslands, peatlands, wetlands, mangroves, savanna
  (within 2 years of entry into force = by June 2025)
- Additional commodities such as maize, sugarcane, charcoal, bio-energy, and others
  (within 2 years)

================================================================
KEY STATISTICS ON EU IMPACT
================================================================

- The EU regulation is projected to reduce carbon emissions by at least 32 million tonnes
  of CO2 equivalent per year
- The EU is one of the world's largest importers of commodities linked to deforestation
- Approximately 10% of global deforestation is driven by EU consumption
