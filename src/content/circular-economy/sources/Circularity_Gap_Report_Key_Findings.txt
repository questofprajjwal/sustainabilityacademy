# Circularity Gap Report Key Findings

## Global Circularity Statistics

Current State (2023 Report):
- Global circularity rate: 7.2% in 2023 (down from 9.1% in 2018 and 8.6% in 2020)
- A 21% decrease over five years in global circularity
- Humanity consumed 500 gigatonnes of materials between 2018-2023, representing 28% of all materials consumed since 1900
- Five of nine key planetary boundaries have been exceeded

## The Circularity Gap

Only 7.2% of materials are cycled back after use. The remaining 92.8% are:
- Dissipated as waste or emissions
- Stored in long-lived products and infrastructure
- Lost from the economic system permanently

## Four Circular Systems

### 1. Agrifood System
- Shift calories from meat/dairy toward cereals, fruits, vegetables, nuts
- Eliminate avoidable food waste through improved logistics
- Promote local, seasonal, organic production
- Mainstream regenerative agriculture practices

### 2. Built Environment
- Maximize energy efficiency with clean energy solutions
- Reuse and renovate existing structures using secondary materials
- Prioritize organic materials (wood, timber, CLT) over steel/concrete
- The built environment accounts for 40% of global emissions

### 3. Manufactured Goods and Consumables
- Replace fast fashion with sustainable, durable textiles
- Implement circular business models (sharing, pay-per-use)
- Extend product lifespans through repair and remanufacturing
- Develop industrial symbiosis

### 4. Mobility and Transport
- Promote car-free lifestyles and cycling infrastructure
- Invest in high-quality public transit
- Minimize air travel, especially long-haul
- Electrify remaining vehicles

## Three-Country Framework (2024 Report)

### Shift Countries (High-Income)
Focus: Reduce material extraction and consumption
- Policy: Strengthen Right to Repair laws; mandate circular building standards
- Finance: Tax material extraction; incentivize sustainable products
- Skills: Integrate circular economy into vocational training

### Grow Countries (Middle-Income)
Focus: Sustainable industrial expansion protecting workers
- Policy: Ban pollution; align incentives with circular manufacturing
- Finance: Direct capital toward green technology

### Build Countries (Low-Income)
Focus: Material use for improved living standards
- Policy: Enable agricultural investment; simplify building codes
- Finance: Secure capital access; support smallholder farmers

## Environmental Impact

Agriculture occupies roughly half of habitable surface and drives:
- One-third of global GHG emissions
- 70% of freshwater withdrawals

A circular economy could:
- Reduce global material extraction by approximately one-third
- Bring human activity within planetary boundaries
- Cut global CO2 emissions significantly

## Key Recommendations

1. Policy: Incentivize circular practices; penalize harmful ones
2. Economics: Adjust fiscal systems to reflect true environmental costs
3. Expertise: Empower workers; distribute circular opportunities equitably
4. Finance: Redirect investment from linear to circular infrastructure

## Report Context

The Circularity Gap Report is produced by Circle Economy and Deloitte.
It tracks the global circularity metric annually to assess progress toward circular economy goals.
The report was first published in 2018, establishing the baseline global circularity rate of 9.1%.
