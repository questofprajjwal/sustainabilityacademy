SFDR 2.0: LEGISLATIVE TIMELINE, PROCESS, AND IMPLEMENTATION
=============================================================
Compiled from official sources and law firm analysis
Research date: March 2026

PART 1: COMPLETE LEGISLATIVE TIMELINE
=======================================

SFDR 1.0 HISTORY (CONTEXT)
-----------------------------
January 2020: SFDR Regulation (EU) 2019/2088 published in Official Journal
March 10, 2021: SFDR Level 1 (entity-level and product-level disclosure framework) enters application
August 2022: Level 2 RTS (Delegated Regulation (EU) 2022/1288) published
January 1, 2023: Full SFDR Level 2 disclosure templates (pre-contractual and periodic) become applicable
November 2022: First wave of Article 8/9 mass reclassifications as managers grapple with "sustainable investment" definition

SFDR REVIEW PROCESS (2022-2025)
---------------------------------
December 2022: Commissioner Mairead McGuinness announces formal SFDR review
September 14, 2023: European Commission launches open and targeted consultations on SFDR assessment
September 14, 2023: Targeted consultation document published
   URL: https://finance.ec.europa.eu/system/files/2023-09/2023-sfdr-implementation-targeted-consultation-document_en.pdf
December 22, 2023: Consultation closes
January 22, 2024: Consultation responses published
May 3, 2024: Summary Report of consultations published
June 18, 2024: Joint ESAs Opinion on SFDR assessment published (JC 2024 06)
   - ESAs propose replacing Articles 8/9 with two categories (Sustainability + Transition) and/or sustainability indicators
   - ESAs recommend abolishing entity-level PAI statements
   - Marks the formal expert recommendation phase
September 2025: ESAs publish Report on PAI disclosures (JC 2025 26)
   URL: https://www.esma.europa.eu/sites/default/files/2025-09/JC_2025_26_Report_on_PAI_disclosures_under_Article_18_SFDR.pdf
November 20, 2025: European Commission publishes formal SFDR 2.0 legislative proposal
   Document: COM(2025) 841 final
   URL: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:52025PC0841
   This is the formal start of the EU legislative (co-decision) procedure

CURRENT LEGISLATIVE STAGE (AS OF MARCH 2026)
----------------------------------------------
The SFDR 2.0 proposal is currently before the European Parliament and the Council of the EU for the "ordinary legislative procedure" (co-decision):

Step 1: Commission proposal (DONE - November 20, 2025)
Step 2: European Parliament first reading
   - Relevant Committee: Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs (ECON)
   - Rapporteur assignment: To be confirmed
   - Expected timeline: 12-18 months for first reading position
   - Parliamentary position expected: 2027

Step 3: Council of the EU position (first reading)
   - Working Party on Financial Services examining the proposal
   - Member states must reach a common position (qualified majority voting)
   - Expected timeline: Parallel with Parliament, likely 2027

Step 4: Trilogue negotiations
   - Parliament and Council negotiate to agree a final text
   - Commission facilitates
   - Informal trilogue meetings typically 3-6 months

Step 5: Formal adoption by Parliament and Council
   - Expected: 2027

Step 6: Publication in Official Journal
   - Enters force 20 days after publication
   - Expected: Late 2027

Step 7: Application period (18 months after entry into force)
   - Application date: Expected late 2028 to early 2029
   - This is when financial market participants must comply

DELEGATED ACTS (LEVEL 2) TIMELINE
------------------------------------
After the Level 1 regulation is adopted, the Commission must develop delegated acts (Level 2 RTS) to specify:
- Exact PAI indicators required for product-level disclosure (Articles 7 and 9)
- List of sustainability-related indicators available for voluntary use
- Standardized two-page disclosure templates
- Methodology for 70% threshold calculations
- Details of mandatory exclusion criteria

Expected timeline for Level 2:
- Commission mandates ESAs to draft RTS: Shortly after Level 1 adoption (2027)
- ESA consultation on draft RTS: 2027-2028
- ESA submission of draft RTS to Commission: 2028
- Commission review and adoption: 2028-2029
- Application: Concurrent with or shortly after Level 1 application date

KEY UNCERTAINTY: The 18-month application period for Level 1 may not provide enough time for Level 2 RTS to be finalized before Level 1 applies. This creates the risk of a gap where Level 1 obligations apply but detailed specifications in Level 2 are not yet available (similar to the SFDR 1.0 problem where Level 1 applied from March 2021 but Level 2 templates only applied from January 2023).

PART 2: COMPARISON WITH PARALLEL REGULATORY PROCESSES
=======================================================

EU OMNIBUS PACKAGE (2025)
--------------------------
On February 26, 2025, the European Commission published its "Omnibus" legislative package aimed at simplifying EU sustainability reporting requirements. This package proposed significant changes to:
- CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive): Proposed to narrow scope, delay application for some companies
- CS3D (Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive): Proposed to delay and modify
- EU Taxonomy Regulation: Proposed modifications to safe harbour rules

RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN OMNIBUS AND SFDR 2.0:
The SFDR 2.0 proposal was developed partly in anticipation of the Omnibus changes. Key interdependencies:
- SFDR 2.0 removes entity-level PAI obligations partly because CSRD was expected to cover this — but CSRD narrowing under Omnibus may reduce the number of companies reporting under CSRD
- The 15% Taxonomy "deemed compliance" pathway in SFDR 2.0 relies on the EU Taxonomy framework, which is also being reviewed
- If CSRD is narrowed significantly, the data available for SFDR product-level disclosures may be reduced
- Industry groups have noted that the coherence between SFDR 2.0 and the Omnibus changes needs to be carefully monitored

ESMA FUND NAMES GUIDELINES (2024)
-----------------------------------
In May 2024, ESMA published final guidelines on fund names using ESG or sustainability-related terms (ESMA34-1983095321-916).
- These guidelines establish minimum thresholds for using sustainability-related terms in fund names
- They apply from November 2024 for new funds and from May 2025 for existing funds
- SFDR 2.0 will ultimately supersede these guidelines once it enters application
- During the transition period (2024-2028), the ESMA guidelines continue to apply

Key ESMA guidelines requirements:
- Funds using "ESG" or sustainability terms must have at least 80% of investments meeting ESG characteristics
- Funds using "sustainable" or similar terms have higher thresholds
- "Transition" terminology requires investing in companies demonstrating transition pathways
- These mirror some of the SFDR 2.0 category criteria but are applied through naming rules, not a formal categorization regime

PART 3: IMPLEMENTATION CONSIDERATIONS FOR FINANCIAL MARKET PARTICIPANTS
========================================================================

CLOSED-ENDED FUND EXEMPTION
-----------------------------
Products that are:
- Closed-ended (not accepting new subscriptions)
- Created AND fully distributed before the SFDR 2.0 application date
...are exempt from the new categorization requirements.

Practical implications:
- These funds can continue using their current SFDR 1.0 classification and disclosures for their remaining life
- They do NOT need to be reclassified under the new Article 7/8/9 categories
- They do NOT need to meet the 70% thresholds
- They DO retain their contractual reporting obligations to existing investors (unless investors agree to modification)

Managers should identify which of their products qualify for this exemption and document the basis for exemption.

OPEN-ENDED AND NEW FUND CONSIDERATIONS
----------------------------------------
For all open-ended funds and any new funds launched after the application date:
- No grace period or transition relief
- Must comply with SFDR 2.0 from day one of application
- Existing Article 8/9 classifications simply cease to exist
- Managers face a binary choice: qualify for a new category or become non-categorized (Article 6a)

CONTRACTUAL OBLIGATIONS
------------------------
A notable point from several law firm analyses:
- Existing investor-facing documents (prospectuses, KIDs, fund rules) make commitments under current SFDR framework
- When SFDR 2.0 replaces SFDR 1.0, existing contractual reporting obligations (e.g., Article 11 periodic reports with Article 9 template format) continue unless:
  - The relevant SFDR obligation itself ceases (which it does for Article 8/9 framework)
  - Or parties mutually agree to modify contractual arrangements
- This creates a transitional complexity: managers may need to communicate with investors about the change in regulatory framework and how fund strategies will be reclassified

REGULATORY INTERACTION SUMMARY
--------------------------------
SFDR 2.0 interacts with these other frameworks:

EU Taxonomy Regulation:
- "Deemed compliance" pathways in Articles 7 and 9 rely on Taxonomy alignment
- Taxonomy alignment is no longer universally required to disclose (only when relied upon)
- The social taxonomy (still incomplete) would expand the scope of qualifying investments for Article 9

CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive):
- Main source of primary sustainability data for institutional investors
- SFDR 2.0 product-level disclosures rely on data produced by CSRD-reporting companies
- CSRD data (available through ESAP - European Single Access Point) should align with SFDR indicator requirements
- Omnibus Package proposed narrowing CSRD scope, which could reduce available data

MiFID II / IDD:
- Suitability requirements for financial advisers remain; sustainability preferences of clients must be assessed
- Financial advisers and portfolio managers are removed from SFDR but not from sustainability-related obligations under MiFID II/IDD
- The category names (Transition, ESG Basics, Sustainable) may be referenced in MiFID suitability assessments

PRIIPs (Packaged Retail and Insurance-based Investment Products):
- Two-page SFDR 2.0 disclosure template and PRIIPs Key Information Document have some overlap
- Commission has stated intent to improve consistency between the two frameworks

AIFMD and UCITS:
- Alternative Investment Fund Managers and UCITS management companies remain in SFDR scope
- Product categorization rules apply to AIFs and UCITS products equally

PART 4: KEY POLICY DEBATES STILL UNRESOLVED (AS OF MARCH 2026)
================================================================

1. ESG BASICS (ARTICLE 8) FOSSIL FUEL EXCLUSIONS
   - The ESG Basics category does NOT exclude companies developing new fossil fuel projects
   - NGOs and climate groups argue this creates a "greenwashing" loophole
   - Parliament may push to tighten exclusions for all three categories during trilogue
   - Watch point: Whether Parliament will insert PAB or CTB exclusions into Article 8

2. STEWARDSHIP AND ENGAGEMENT
   - The proposal does not specifically require active ownership or engagement strategies
   - IIGCC and some institutional investors want mandatory stewardship requirements as part of category criteria
   - Debate: Whether engagement should be a qualifying strategy for the 70% threshold in Article 7 (Transition)

3. MANDATORY VS. OPTIONAL CATEGORIZATION
   - Currently: Categories are mandatory for any fund making sustainability claims
   - Some argue the system should be voluntary (opt-in) to give managers more flexibility
   - Counter-argument: Voluntary systems create inconsistency and reduce comparability

4. IMPACT CATEGORY CRITERIA
   - The "impact" sub-category for Articles 7 and 9 is supported by the proposal but specifics are left to delegated acts
   - Industry wants clear criteria for what qualifies as "pre-defined, positive, measurable impact"
   - Risk: Vague criteria could undermine the impact label's credibility

5. PRIVATE MARKETS DATA
   - No special provisions for private equity, venture capital, infrastructure, or real estate
   - These asset classes often cannot access the same quality of ESG data as listed equities
   - Industry pushing for specific delegated act guidance on alternative asset classes

6. SMALL AND MEDIUM-SIZED ASSET MANAGERS
   - While entity-level PAI obligations are removed, product-level obligations for categorized products remain complex
   - Smaller managers may face disproportionate compliance burden relative to resources
   - Push for simplified compliance pathways for smaller FMPs
