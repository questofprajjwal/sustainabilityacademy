EUROPEAN COMMISSION 2023 SFDR CONSULTATION - KEY FINDINGS
==========================================================
Title: Open and Targeted Consultations on the SFDR Assessment
Launch date: 14 September 2023
Close date: 22 December 2023
Summary report published: 3 May 2024
Administering department: DG FISMA (Financial Stability, Financial Services and Capital Markets Union)

Official consultation page:
https://finance.ec.europa.eu/regulation-and-supervision/consultations/finance-2023-sfdr-implementation_en

Official consultation document (targeted):

BACKGROUND
----------
Commissioner Mairead McGuinness announced in December 2022 that the Commission would conduct a comprehensive review of the SFDR, examining:
- Legal certainty and clarity of key concepts
- Useability of the regulation for investors and financial market participants
- The SFDR's ability to combat greenwashing

The Commission ran two parallel consultations:
1. Open (public) consultation: Targeting the general public, individual investors, and non-specialized stakeholders
2. Targeted consultation: Targeting public bodies, financial market participants, professional investors, NGOs, national regulators, and entities with specialized knowledge of SFDR and sustainable finance

SCOPE OF THE CONSULTATION
--------------------------
The consultations covered:
- Current SFDR disclosure requirements and their effectiveness
- Interaction of SFDR with other sustainable finance legislation (EU Taxonomy, CSRD, MiFID II, IDD, PRIIPs, AIFMD, UCITS)
- Potential changes to disclosure requirements for financial market participants at entity level
- Potential changes to product-level disclosure requirements
- Potential establishment of a product categorization system
- Interaction with national sustainable finance frameworks

KEY QUANTITATIVE FINDINGS FROM THE SUMMARY REPORT (MAY 2024)
-------------------------------------------------------------
296 respondents answered the section on SFDR interactions with other sustainable finance regulations.

On relevance of SFDR objectives:
- 89% considered that the objective of strengthening transparency through sustainability-related disclosures is STILL RELEVANT
- 94% agreed that an EU-level disclosure framework is MORE EFFECTIVE than national measures

On problems with current SFDR:
- 77% highlighted key limitations including:
  - Lack of legal clarity regarding key concepts (especially "sustainable investment" definition)
  - Limited relevance of certain disclosure requirements
  - Data availability issues (especially for PAI indicators)

On Articles 8 and 9 as de facto labels:
- Stakeholders overwhelmingly confirmed that Articles 8 and 9 have become informal product labels in the market
- Strong consensus that this was unintended by the original SFDR but has created significant greenwashing risks
- Majority supported introducing a formal product categorization system to replace the informal labeling

On PAI disclosures:
- Entity-level PAI statements were criticized as burdensome and duplicative with emerging CSRD requirements
- Product-level PAI templates were considered too technical and data-heavy for retail investors
- Many stakeholders called for alignment of SFDR data requirements with CSRD reporting to reduce duplication

On disclosure templates:
- Current templates were widely considered too long, too technical, and not investor-friendly
- Strong support for shorter, more targeted disclosures focused on a smaller number of key indicators
- Support for standardized, comparable formats enabling investor comparison across products

On the "sustainable investment" definition:
- Near-universal criticism of the lack of clear thresholds and prescriptive criteria
- Major market participants had developed widely divergent methodologies for determining what qualifies as a "sustainable investment"
- Strong support for either abolishing the definition or replacing it with prescriptive, comparable standards

On interaction with EU Taxonomy:
- Requiring Article 8 and 9 funds to disclose Taxonomy alignment percentages was seen as problematic
- Reporting "0%" alignment frustrated investors and created misleading impressions
- Many called for mandatory Taxonomy reporting only where Taxonomy criteria are genuinely used in the investment strategy

On costs:
- Financial market participants reported significant compliance costs from the SFDR Level 2 RTS templates
- Entity-level PAI statements in particular were called out as resource-intensive with limited investor value
- Industry groups cited annual compliance costs in the hundreds of millions of euros across the EU fund industry

INDUSTRY POSITIONS AT TIME OF CONSULTATION (SEPTEMBER-DECEMBER 2023)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Asset management associations (EFAMA, ICMA, etc.):
- Generally supportive of product categorization to replace de facto Article 8/9 labels
- Pushed for clear, prescriptive criteria that could be applied consistently across fund types
- Concerns about data availability, particularly for non-listed assets and alternative investments
- Requested adequate transition periods and regulatory certainty

NGOs and civil society (WWF, ShareAction, ClientEarth, etc.):
- Supported ambitious categorization standards to prevent greenwashing
- Called for meaningful minimum sustainability thresholds for each category
- Concerned about the risk of a broad "middle" category becoming a new greenwashing vehicle
- Pushed for mandatory exclusion of fossil fuels from all sustainability categories

National regulators (AMF, BaFin, etc.):
- Mixed views on scope changes (some wanted to retain financial advisers in scope)
- Generally supportive of harmonized standards to prevent regulatory fragmentation
- Some member states (notably France, Luxembourg) had developed national frameworks they wanted preserved

Retail investor advocates:
- Strong support for simplified, comparable disclosures accessible to non-expert investors
- Support for a clear labeling regime similar to EU energy performance certificates
- Concern that the current SFDR provides little meaningful information to retail investors

CONTEXT: HOW THE 2023 CONSULTATION LED TO SFDR 2.0
---------------------------------------------------
The consultation results formed the evidence base for the Commission's formal legislative proposal of November 2025. Key consultation conclusions that were directly translated into the SFDR 2.0 proposal:

1. Consultation finding: Articles 8/9 are de facto labels and a formal categorization system is needed
   => SFDR 2.0 response: Three mandatory product categories (Articles 7, 8, 9)

2. Consultation finding: The "sustainable investment" definition lacks clarity and allows inconsistent application
   => SFDR 2.0 response: Concept entirely removed and replaced with category-specific criteria

3. Consultation finding: Entity-level PAI disclosures overlap with CSRD and should be rationalized
   => SFDR 2.0 response: Entity-level PAI statements abolished

4. Consultation finding: Disclosure templates are too long and complex
   => SFDR 2.0 response: Two-page maximum for pre-contractual disclosures

5. Consultation finding: Mandatory Taxonomy reporting creates confusion when funds report "0%" alignment
   => SFDR 2.0 response: Taxonomy reporting only required where Taxonomy pathways are used

6. Consultation finding: Financial advisers should potentially be removed from scope
   => SFDR 2.0 response: Financial advisers and portfolio managers removed from SFDR scope

7. Consultation finding: Naming and marketing restrictions needed for non-sustainability products
   => SFDR 2.0 response: Strict naming and marketing restrictions for non-categorized products
