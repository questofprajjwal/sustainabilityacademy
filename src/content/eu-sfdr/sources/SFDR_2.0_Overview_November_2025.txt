SFDR 2.0: EUROPEAN COMMISSION PROPOSAL - NOVEMBER 20, 2025
==========================================================
Source: European Commission COM(2025) 841 final, published 20 November 2025
EUR-Lex reference: CELEX:52025PC0841
Official PDF: https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=CELEX:52025PC0841

OVERVIEW
--------
On 20 November 2025, the European Commission published its formal legislative proposal to revise the Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR), commonly referred to as "SFDR 2.0." The proposal fundamentally restructures the SFDR framework from a disclosure-based regime into a mandatory product categorization (labeling) regime with minimum sustainability standards.

The Commission estimates SFDR 2.0 will reduce annual compliance costs for financial market participants by approximately 25%, including savings of around EUR 56 million annually from eliminating entity-level PAI statements.

BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE
-------------------------
The original SFDR (Regulation (EU) 2019/2088), which entered into application on 10 March 2021, was designed as a disclosure framework — not a product labeling system. However, in practice, the market informally treated Article 8 ("light green") and Article 9 ("dark green") as de facto labels, leading to widespread confusion and greenwashing risks.

Key problems identified with SFDR 1.0:
- Disclosure templates became excessively long and technical, averaging six pages or more
- Article 8 was used by funds with minimal sustainability commitments, including some fossil fuel funds
- The "sustainable investment" definition was so flexible that interpretations diverged significantly across the industry
- The "do no significant harm" (DNSH) principle and "good governance" tests were difficult to apply consistently
- Entity-level PAI disclosures duplicated requirements now covered by the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD)
- Retail investors found disclosures incomprehensible
- 77% of respondents to the 2023 consultation highlighted lack of legal clarity, limited relevance of certain disclosures, and data availability issues

The September 2023 to December 2023 open and targeted consultations received responses confirming that 89% of respondents considered transparency-related disclosure objectives still relevant, and 94% agreed EU-level rules are more effective than national measures — but the mechanics needed fundamental reform.

FUNDAMENTAL STRUCTURAL CHANGE: FROM DISCLOSURE TO CATEGORIZATION
-----------------------------------------------------------------
SFDR 2.0 introduces a mandatory product categorization regime to replace the current disclosure-only framework. The three new categories replace the informal Article 6/8/9 structure:

OLD SYSTEM (SFDR 1.0):
- Article 6: Products not promoting sustainability (but with mandatory disclosure of how sustainability risks are integrated)
- Article 8: Products that "promote environmental or social characteristics" (light green)
- Article 9: Products with "sustainable investment as their objective" (dark green)

NEW SYSTEM (SFDR 2.0):
- Article 6a: Non-sustainability-related products (with strict limits on ESG claims)
- Article 7: Transition Category
- Article 8: ESG Basics Category
- Article 9: Sustainable Category
- Article 9a: Combination/fund-of-funds category
- Impact sub-category: Available for Articles 7 and 9 products meeting additional criteria

THE THREE NEW PRODUCT CATEGORIES
---------------------------------

ARTICLE 7 - TRANSITION CATEGORY
Purpose: Financing the shift of companies, activities, or assets toward sustainability

Investment Threshold:
- Minimum 70% of investments must meet clear, measurable transition objectives
- Alternative pathway: 15% Taxonomy-aligned investments (deemed compliance; subject to review after 36 months)
- Alignment with EU Climate Transition Benchmark (CTB) automatically qualifies

Eligible investments for the 70% include:
- Taxonomy-aligned economic activities
- Transitional economic activities under the EU Taxonomy
- Businesses with credible five-year plans for Taxonomy alignment
- Investments with credible transition plans or science-based targets
- Investments in companies targeted through engagement strategies
- Other investments with "proper justification"

Mandatory Exclusions:
- All Climate Transition Benchmark (CTB) exclusions apply, including:
  - Controversial weapons manufacturers
  - Tobacco companies
  - Companies in violation of UN Global Compact / OECD Guidelines for MNEs
  - Companies deriving 1% or more revenue from hard coal and lignite
- Additionally excluded: companies developing new fossil fuel exploration, extraction, or distribution projects

PAI Disclosure: Products in the Transition Category MUST identify and disclose principal adverse impacts of their investments on sustainability factors (qualitatively or quantitatively), along with explanations of remedial actions taken.

---

ARTICLE 8 - ESG BASICS CATEGORY
Purpose: Integrating sustainability factors into investment strategy beyond pure risk management

Investment Threshold:
- Minimum 70% must integrate sustainability factors using "appropriate sustainability-related indicators"
- Qualifying approaches include: investments with above-average ESG ratings, positive sustainability track records, or combinations of Articles 7, 8, and 9 investments
- Includes "other investments" integrating sustainability factors with "proper justification"

Mandatory Exclusions:
- Climate Transition Benchmark exclusions apply:
  - Controversial weapons
  - Tobacco
  - UN Global Compact / OECD violations
  - Companies deriving 1%+ revenue from hard coal and lignite
- Note: ESG Basics does NOT exclude companies developing new fossil fuel projects (unlike Transition and Sustainable categories)

PAI Disclosure: NOT mandatory for ESG Basics Category products (unlike Articles 7 and 9)

---

ARTICLE 9 - SUSTAINABLE CATEGORY
Purpose: Investing in companies or assets already aligned with environmental or social sustainability objectives

Investment Threshold:
- Minimum 70% must meet clear, measurable sustainability objectives
- Alternative pathway: 15% Taxonomy-aligned investments (deemed compliance; subject to review after 36 months)
- Alignment with EU Paris-Aligned Benchmark (PAB) automatically qualifies

Eligible investments for the 70% include:
- Taxonomy-aligned economic activities
- Issuance of EU Green Bonds
- EU-backed financing mechanisms
- European social entrepreneurship funds
- Other assets contributing to sustainability objectives with "proper justification"

Mandatory Exclusions (STRICTEST of the three categories):
- All Paris-Aligned Benchmark (PAB) exclusions apply, which include everything in the CTB plus:
  - Companies excluded from Paris-Aligned Benchmarks for fossil fuel exposure
  - Companies deriving more than 10% revenue from oil fuels
  - Companies deriving more than 50% revenue from electricity generation exceeding 100g CO2e/kWh
  - Hard coal/lignite phase-out non-compliance

PAI Disclosure: Products in the Sustainable Category MUST identify and disclose principal adverse impacts of investments on sustainability factors, along with remedial action explanations.

---

ARTICLE 9a - COMBINATION CATEGORY
Permits funds of funds and indirect investment strategies to qualify for a category if they achieve the 70% thresholds through investments in other categorized products (Articles 7, 8, or 9). This ensures fund-of-fund structures are not disadvantaged by the new framework.

---

IMPACT SUB-CATEGORY
Available to products within Articles 7 or 9 that demonstrate pre-defined, positive, measurable social or environmental impact. Must be supported by an impact theory of change and robust impact measurement provisions. These products can use the term "impact" in names and marketing.

---

NON-CATEGORIZED PRODUCTS (ARTICLE 6a)
Products that do not meet any of the three category criteria face strict restrictions:
- CANNOT make sustainability-related claims in names or marketing materials
- CANNOT use terms like "sustainable," "transition," "ESG," "green," or equivalent in product names
- May only include voluntary sustainability information if it constitutes LESS THAN 10% of the investment strategy description in pre-contractual documents
- The 10% cap prevents sustainability information from becoming a "central element" of the fund's pitch

REMOVAL OF KEY CONCEPTS
-------------------------
SFDR 2.0 entirely removes or significantly modifies several concepts from SFDR 1.0:

1. "Sustainable investment" definition: REMOVED entirely
   - Under SFDR 1.0, "sustainable investments" were investments in economic activities contributing to an environmental or social objective, subject to DNSH and good governance tests
   - The definition was widely criticized for inconsistent application and a lack of clear thresholds
   - SFDR 2.0 replaces this with specific category-level criteria

2. "Do no significant harm" (DNSH) principle: REMOVED from SFDR context
   - Still exists within EU Taxonomy framework but no longer imported into SFDR product-level assessments
   - Replaced by mandatory exclusion lists at the category level

3. "Good governance" test: REMOVED
   - Previously required investee companies to follow "sound management structures, employee relations, remuneration of staff, and tax compliance"
   - Replaced by reference to UN Global Compact and OECD Guidelines compliance in exclusion criteria

CHANGES TO PRINCIPAL ADVERSE IMPACT (PAI) DISCLOSURES
------------------------------------------------------
SFDR 1.0 PAI Framework:
- Entity-level: Financial market participants (FMPs) with more than 500 employees were REQUIRED to publish a Principal Adverse Impact Statement at the organizational level (Article 4)
- Smaller FMPs: Had to comply-or-explain regarding PAI consideration
- Product-level: Article 7 of SFDR 1.0 required PAI disclosure at product level for funds with sustainable investment objectives

SFDR 2.0 PAI Changes:
- ENTITY-LEVEL PAI STATEMENTS: ABOLISHED entirely
  - Rationale: Overlap with CSRD, which already requires entity-level sustainability reporting for large companies
  - Expected annual saving: approximately EUR 56 million across the industry
  - Large asset managers will continue to report sustainability information at entity level under CSRD
- PRODUCT-LEVEL PAI: RETAINED and MANDATORY for Articles 7 and 9
  - Article 7 (Transition) and Article 9 (Sustainable) products must identify and disclose PAIs of their portfolio investments on sustainability factors
  - Must explain what remedial actions are being taken
  - Can be qualitative or quantitative
  - ESG Basics (Article 8) products are NOT required to disclose PAIs
- ENTITY-LEVEL REMUNERATION DISCLOSURE: ABOLISHED (was Article 5 under SFDR 1.0)

SUSTAINABILITY-RELATED INDICATORS
----------------------------------
SFDR 2.0 introduces the concept of "sustainability-related indicators" as a core measurement tool at the product level:

- Each categorized product must use "appropriate sustainability-related indicator(s)" to measure:
  - Adherence to the product's stated sustainability strategy
  - Progress made toward stated objectives

- The Commission is empowered to produce Regulatory Technical Standards (RTS) specifying PAI indicators and sustainability indicators for voluntary use

- Unlike the current mandatory list of PAI indicators, the new approach allows flexibility for different asset classes and strategies while maintaining comparability through standardized templates

DISCLOSURE SIMPLIFICATION
--------------------------
Current SFDR 1.0 disclosure requirements result in templates often exceeding six pages and are widely considered too technical for retail investors.

SFDR 2.0 disclosure architecture:
- Pre-contractual disclosures: Limited to MAXIMUM TWO PAGES for Articles 7, 8, and 9 products
- Impact sub-category: Additional ONE PAGE disclosure template permitted
- Website disclosures: Products may simply reference pre-contractual documents rather than publishing additional content (current Article 10 eliminated)
- Periodic reporting: Continues but in streamlined format

Mandatory content for all three category disclosures:
1. Statement that the product meets conditions for the stated category
2. Description of the relevant sustainability strategy
3. Sustainability-related indicator(s) used to measure adherence and progress
4. How the 70% threshold is met (or how deemed-compliance pathways are used)
5. Applicable mandatory exclusions
6. Key performance indicators

"Deemed compliance" routes:
- Products referencing EU Climate Transition Benchmarks automatically qualify for Article 7
- Products referencing EU Paris-Aligned Benchmarks automatically qualify for Article 9
- Products with at least 15% Taxonomy-aligned investments are "deemed" to meet the 70% contribution test (subject to review after 36 months)

CHANGES TO SCOPE OF SFDR
--------------------------
SFDR 2.0 narrows the scope of who is covered:

REMOVED FROM SCOPE:
- Financial advisers (previously subject to Article 13 entity-level disclosures and product-level disclosures when making recommendations)
- Credit institutions and investment firms providing portfolio management services
- Rationale: These entities do not manufacture financial products; their sustainability claims are now addressed through MiFID II and IDD suitability frameworks

RETAINED IN SCOPE:
- UCITS management companies
- AIFMs (Alternative Investment Fund Managers)
- Insurance companies (for insurance-based investment products / IBIPs)
- Pension providers (for pan-European Personal Pension Products / PEPPs)
- EuVECA and EuSEF managers

TRANSITION EXEMPTIONS:
- Closed-ended funds that were created AND distributed before the SFDR 2.0 application date are exempt from the new categorization requirements
- Insurance-based investment products receive a 12-month transition period
- No general transition period exists for other legacy SFDR 1.0 products — once SFDR 2.0 applies, existing Article 8/9 classifications simply cease

NAMING AND MARKETING RESTRICTIONS
-----------------------------------
One of the most significant operational changes:

- Only products meeting Article 7, 8, or 9 criteria may use sustainability-related claims in:
  - Fund names
  - Marketing communications
  - Prospectuses
  - Any investor-facing materials

- Non-categorized (Article 6a) products CANNOT use sustainability terminology in names or marketing

- Reference to the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (2005/29/EC) and the Consumer Empowerment Directive (2024/825/EU) for green transition: marketing of "ESG claims" must be fair, clear, and not misleading

- The proposal specifically states that sustainability terminology cannot appear in the name of any non-categorized product

TAXONOMY ALIGNMENT DISCLOSURES
--------------------------------
Current requirement: All Article 8 and 9 products must disclose the percentage of their investments that are EU Taxonomy-aligned (or explain why they are not).

SFDR 2.0 change:
- Taxonomy alignment disclosures are only mandatory when a product explicitly relies on Taxonomy-aligned criteria to meet its category requirements (i.e., using the 15% deemed-compliance pathway)
- Products not using the Taxonomy pathway are no longer required to report Taxonomy alignment percentages
- This addresses a major pain point where funds were required to report "0%" Taxonomy alignment, which retail investors found confusing and misleading

ENTITY-LEVEL DISCLOSURES (RESIDUAL)
-------------------------------------
What remains at entity level under SFDR 2.0:
- Policies on integrating sustainability risks into investment processes
- Potential impacts of sustainability risks on financial returns
- These are narrowed compared to current SFDR 1.0 Article 3 (sustainability risk policies) and Article 4 (PAI)

What is removed:
- Entity-level PAI statements (Article 4)
- Comply-or-explain for smaller FMPs on PAI
- Remuneration policy disclosures regarding sustainability risk integration (Article 5)

DATA AND METHODOLOGY REQUIREMENTS
-----------------------------------
SFDR 2.0 formalizes requirements for:
- Documented arrangements with external ESG data providers
- Estimation methodologies when primary data is unavailable
- Client access to data sourcing information upon request
- Transparency on the use of third-party data and proxy methodologies

MAXIMUM HARMONIZATION
-----------------------
SFDR 2.0 adopts a "maximum harmonization" approach, which means:
- Individual EU/EEA member states CANNOT impose additional sustainability disclosure requirements on top of SFDR 2.0
- This prevents regulatory fragmentation across the Single Market
- Countries like France, which had imposed additional domestic requirements, will need to align

IMPLEMENTATION TIMELINE
------------------------
- November 20, 2025: European Commission published the legislative proposal (COM(2025) 841 final)
- Current status (early 2026): Proposal is with the European Parliament and Council for co-decision (trilogue) process
- Expected timeline for co-decision: Approximately 18-24 months of negotiations
- Entry into force: 20 days after publication in the Official Journal of the EU (expected 2027)
- Application date: 18 months after entry into force (earliest realistic: late 2028 or early 2029)
- "Start-up period": The Commission has indicated a 2027-2028 orientation period
- Closed-end fund exemption deadline: Products "created and distributed" before the application date are exempt

MARKET IMPACT PROJECTIONS (Morningstar Sustainalytics Analysis)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Current distribution of EU fund assets:
- Article 8 funds: approximately 56% of EU AUM
- Article 9 funds: approximately 2.7% of EU AUM
- Article 6 funds: approximately 41% of EU AUM

Projected distribution under SFDR 2.0:
- ESG Basics (Article 8): 32%-41% of assets (significant reduction from current 56%)
- Sustainable (Article 9): 4.5%-7% of assets (roughly doubling from 2.7%)
- Transition (Article 7): 1.6%-3% of assets (small but new category)
- Non-categorized (Article 6a): 52%-70% of assets (significant increase from current 41%)

Key implication: A large proportion of current Article 8 funds are expected to either downgrade to non-categorized status or face significant portfolio restructuring to maintain a sustainability category.

COMPARISON WITH UK SDR
-----------------------
There is a notable parallel between SFDR 2.0 and the UK's Sustainability Disclosure Requirements (SDR), which also introduced a product labeling system. However:
- UK SDR is goal-based with four voluntary labels (Sustainability Focus, Sustainability Improvers, Sustainability Impact, Sustainability Mixed Goals)
- EU SFDR 2.0 is hierarchical with three mandatory categories
- Cross-regime mapping will be complex for managers operating in both markets
- The 70% portfolio threshold in SFDR 2.0 aligns broadly with UK SDR's minimum 70% threshold requirement
