SFDR 2.0: DETAILED ANALYSIS OF PAI CHANGES AND DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
========================================================================
Compiled from official proposal (COM(2025) 841 final) and law firm analysis
Research date: March 2026

PART 1: PRINCIPAL ADVERSE IMPACT (PAI) DISCLOSURES - COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS
=============================================================================

CURRENT PAI FRAMEWORK UNDER SFDR 1.0
--------------------------------------
Articles 4, 7, and associated Level 2 RTS (Annex I) of SFDR 1.0 establish the PAI framework:

ENTITY-LEVEL PAI (Article 4 of SFDR 1.0):
- Applies to: All financial market participants (FMPs)
- Large FMPs (>500 employees): MANDATORY annual publication of a Principal Adverse Impact (PAI) Statement on their website
- Small FMPs (<500 employees): Comply-or-explain (must state on website whether they do or do not consider PAIs, and if not, explain why)
- Content: Must describe:
  - Policies for identifying and prioritizing principal adverse impacts
  - Description of principal adverse impacts and actions taken to address them
  - Engagement policies
  - Reference to responsible business conduct codes adhered to
- Reference period: Annual, calendar year
- Due date: Published by 30 June each year

PRODUCT-LEVEL PAI (Article 7 of SFDR 1.0):
- Applies to: Financial products with "sustainable investment" as their objective (Article 9 products)
- Describes how the financial product considers PAIs of investment decisions on sustainability factors
- Must use specific PAI indicators from the Level 2 RTS

THE 64 PAI INDICATORS (Level 2 RTS, Delegated Regulation (EU) 2022/1288):
The current RTS specifies a comprehensive list of PAI indicators divided into:

MANDATORY INDICATORS (14 indicators for investee companies, plus indicators for sovereigns/real estate):
Environmental Indicators:
1. GHG emissions (Scope 1)
2. GHG emissions (Scope 2)
3. GHG emissions (Scope 3 where available / total GHG emissions)
4. Carbon footprint
5. GHG intensity of investee companies
6. Fossil fuel sector exposure
7. Non-renewable energy consumption and production
8. Energy consumption intensity per high-impact sector
9. Activities negatively affecting biodiversity-sensitive areas
10. Emissions to water
11. Hazardous waste ratio

Social and Governance Indicators:
12. Violations of UN Global Compact principles and OECD guidelines
13. Lack of processes and mechanisms to monitor UNGC/OECD compliance
14. Unadjusted gender pay gap
15. Board gender diversity
16. Exposure to controversial weapons (anti-personnel mines, cluster munitions, chemical/biological weapons)

OPTIONAL INDICATORS (46 additional indicators):
Additional environmental indicators (e.g., excessive water use, deforestation, high-impact land use)
Additional social indicators (e.g., excessive CEO pay ratio, insufficient whistleblowing protection, lack of supply chain due diligence)

CRITICISMS OF CURRENT PAI FRAMEWORK:
- The 14 mandatory indicators plus 46 optional indicators create an extensive data collection burden
- Many indicators (especially Scope 3, biodiversity, water data) are unavailable from primary sources for many investments
- Data quality varies significantly across asset classes (listed equities have better coverage; private markets have very poor coverage)
- Entity-level aggregation of PAI indicators across entire portfolios is methodologically complex
- The resulting disclosures are typically long tables of numbers that are not meaningful to retail investors
- Smaller asset managers often lack the resources to collect and process PAI data at scale
- There is insufficient standardization in how PAI indicators are calculated even where data exists

HOW SFDR 2.0 CHANGES PAI DISCLOSURES
---------------------------------------

CHANGE 1: ENTITY-LEVEL PAI STATEMENTS ABOLISHED
- Articles 4 and 5 of SFDR 1.0 (entity-level PAI and remuneration disclosures) are DELETED
- Rationale:
  a) Overlap with CSRD: The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) already requires large companies (including large asset managers) to report on how their business activities affect sustainability factors
  b) Duplication reduction: Having both SFDR entity-level PAI and CSRD sustainability reporting was creating redundant compliance work
  c) Cost savings: Estimated EUR 56 million per year in savings across the EU industry
- Impact: Even large asset managers with >500 employees no longer need to publish annual entity-level PAI statements under SFDR
- Note: These managers will still report sustainability information at entity level under CSRD, but through CSRD mechanisms, not SFDR

CHANGE 2: PRODUCT-LEVEL PAI RETAINED FOR ARTICLES 7 AND 9 ONLY
- Article 7 (Transition) products: MUST identify and disclose PAIs of investments on sustainability factors
  - Can be qualitative OR quantitative disclosure
  - Must include explanation of remedial actions being taken
  - Specific indicators to be specified in delegated acts

- Article 9 (Sustainable) products: MUST identify and disclose PAIs of investments on sustainability factors
  - Same requirements as Article 7
  - Higher overall sustainability standards mean PAI identification is especially relevant

- Article 8 (ESG Basics) products: NO mandatory PAI disclosure at product level
  - This is a significant relaxation vs. SFDR 1.0 where some Article 8 products had to consider PAIs

CHANGE 3: STREAMLINED PAI INDICATOR REQUIREMENTS
- The Commission is empowered to specify PAI indicators and sustainability-related indicators via delegated acts
- This replaces the current fixed list of 14 mandatory plus 46 optional indicators
- Expected direction: Fewer, more material indicators aligned with data available from CSRD-reporting entities
- "Appropriate" indicators: Each product can select indicators relevant to its strategy (with flexibility)
- Focus shifts from comprehensive indicator lists to targeted, strategy-relevant indicators

CHANGE 4: RECOGNITION OF ESTIMATES AND THIRD-PARTY DATA
- SFDR 2.0 formally recognizes that:
  - Estimates may be necessary when primary data is unavailable
  - Third-party ESG data providers can be used
- BUT: Requires formalized, documented arrangements with external data providers
- Managers must explain estimation methodologies
- Clients can request documentation of data sources and calculation methods
- This addresses a major practical gap in SFDR 1.0 where data estimates were used widely but not formally addressed

PART 2: PRODUCT-LEVEL DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS - DETAILED BREAKDOWN
====================================================================

CURRENT SFDR 1.0 DISCLOSURE STRUCTURE
--------------------------------------
SFDR 1.0 requires disclosures at three points:
1. Pre-contractual (at point of sale / in prospectus): Articles 6, 8, 9 disclosures
2. On website: Article 10 disclosures (ongoing product information)
3. Periodic reports (annual): Article 11 disclosures (performance reporting)

Current template length:
- Article 8 template: Typically 4-6 pages
- Article 9 template: Typically 5-7 pages
- Annex I-IV of Level 2 RTS specifies mandatory template formats

SFDR 2.0 NEW DISCLOSURE STRUCTURE
-----------------------------------
PRE-CONTRACTUAL DISCLOSURES:
- Maximum length: TWO PAGES for Articles 7, 8, and 9 products
- Additional ONE PAGE for "impact" sub-category variant
- Must include as mandatory minimum:
  1. Statement that the product meets the conditions for the stated category (Article 7, 8, or 9)
  2. Description of the sustainability strategy pursued
  3. Sustainability-related indicator(s) used to measure adherence to the strategy and progress
  4. How the 70% threshold is met (or which "deemed compliance" pathway is used)
  5. Applicable mandatory exclusions
  6. Key performance indicators

WEBSITE DISCLOSURES:
- Current Article 10 (requiring additional website-specific sustainability information) is ABOLISHED
- Products may simply reference pre-contractual documents on their website
- No additional content requirement beyond what is in the two-page pre-contractual disclosure
- Significant simplification for compliance teams managing website content

PERIODIC REPORTS:
- Continue to be required
- Streamlined content: Focus on sustainability-related indicators and progress toward stated objectives
- Must report against the KPIs stated in the pre-contractual document

VOLUNTARY ESG INFORMATION FOR NON-CATEGORIZED PRODUCTS (ARTICLE 6a):
- Non-categorized products CAN include voluntary sustainability information BUT:
  - It must constitute LESS THAN 10% of the investment strategy description in pre-contractual documents
  - Cannot make the product "appear as" a sustainability-related product
  - Cannot use sustainability terminology that resembles Articles 7, 8, or 9 claims
- Purpose: Allow genuinely non-sustainability products to mention ESG considerations without implying a sustainability label

PART 3: SUSTAINABILITY-RELATED INDICATORS - A NEW DISCLOSURE CONCEPT
=====================================================================

WHAT ARE SUSTAINABILITY-RELATED INDICATORS UNDER SFDR 2.0?
-----------------------------------------------------------
This is a new, flexible concept replacing the rigid PAI indicator tables of SFDR 1.0.

Definition implied by proposal:
"Appropriate sustainability-related indicators" are metrics chosen by the financial market participant to:
- Measure how the product's investment portfolio adheres to its stated sustainability strategy
- Track progress toward the product's stated sustainability objectives
- Be disclosed in the two-page pre-contractual template and in periodic reports

KEY CHARACTERISTICS:
1. Not a fixed mandatory list (unlike current 14 mandatory PAI indicators)
2. Must be "appropriate" to the product's strategy and asset class
3. Examples (expected from delegated acts):
   - For Transition products: Carbon intensity reduction trajectory, percentage of assets with credible transition plans
   - For ESG Basics products: Portfolio ESG rating vs. benchmark, PAI scores for key metrics
   - For Sustainable products: Percentage of Taxonomy-aligned investments, alignment with specific UN SDGs
4. Comparable across products through standardized template formats
5. Linked to the category requirements: Must demonstrate how the 70% threshold is met

WHY THIS APPROACH:
- Avoids the one-size-fits-all problem of current mandatory PAI indicators
- Different asset classes (equities, bonds, real estate, infrastructure, private equity) have different relevant sustainability metrics
- Enables innovation in sustainability measurement while maintaining comparability through template standardization
- Aligns with the principle that disclosure should be "targeted and relevant" rather than comprehensive and technical

COMMISSION DELEGATED ACT POWERS:
The Commission is empowered under SFDR 2.0 to specify via delegated acts:
- List of PAI indicators for mandatory product-level disclosure (for Articles 7 and 9)
- List of sustainability-related indicators available for voluntary use
- Standardized template formats for the two-page disclosures
- Methodology guidance for the 70% threshold calculations

This means the full picture of what "appropriate sustainability-related indicators" means in practice will only become clear after delegated acts are published. This is a major source of uncertainty for the industry during 2026-2028.

PART 4: COMPARISON TABLE - SFDR 1.0 vs SFDR 2.0
=================================================

Entity-Level PAI Statement:
- SFDR 1.0: Mandatory for large FMPs (>500 employees); comply-or-explain for small FMPs
- SFDR 2.0: ABOLISHED entirely

Entity-Level Remuneration Disclosure:
- SFDR 1.0: Required under Article 5
- SFDR 2.0: ABOLISHED

Product-Level PAI Disclosure:
- SFDR 1.0: Required for Article 9 products; Article 8 products must explain consideration
- SFDR 2.0: Mandatory for Articles 7 and 9; NOT required for Article 8 (ESG Basics)

Sustainability Categories:
- SFDR 1.0: Article 6 (no claims), Article 8 (promotes ESG), Article 9 (sustainable objective)
- SFDR 2.0: Article 6a (non-sustainability), Article 7 (Transition), Article 8 (ESG Basics), Article 9 (Sustainable)

Minimum Investment Threshold:
- SFDR 1.0: None specified (Article 8); Sustainable investment % varies (Article 9)
- SFDR 2.0: 70% for all three categories (Articles 7, 8, 9)

Mandatory Exclusions:
- SFDR 1.0: None mandatory in SFDR itself (some in EU Climate Benchmark Regulation)
- SFDR 2.0: Mandatory exclusion lists for all three categories (varying strictness)

"Sustainable Investment" Definition:
- SFDR 1.0: Defined in Article 2(17); widely criticized for ambiguity
- SFDR 2.0: REMOVED entirely

"Do No Significant Harm" Principle:
- SFDR 1.0: Required for all sustainable investments
- SFDR 2.0: REMOVED from SFDR (remains in EU Taxonomy)

"Good Governance" Test:
- SFDR 1.0: Required for all sustainable investments
- SFDR 2.0: REMOVED; replaced by reference to UN Global Compact / OECD Guidelines in exclusion criteria

Taxonomy Alignment Reporting:
- SFDR 1.0: Mandatory for all Article 8 and 9 products (even if 0%)
- SFDR 2.0: Only required when product uses Taxonomy criteria for category qualification

Pre-contractual Disclosure Length:
- SFDR 1.0: Typically 4-7 pages (Articles 8/9 templates)
- SFDR 2.0: Maximum 2 pages (+1 page for impact sub-category)

Website Disclosures (Article 10):
- SFDR 1.0: Mandatory additional sustainability information on website
- SFDR 2.0: ABOLISHED; reference to pre-contractual document sufficient

Financial Advisers in Scope:
- SFDR 1.0: Yes (Articles 2, 13)
- SFDR 2.0: REMOVED from scope

Portfolio Managers in Scope:
- SFDR 1.0: Yes (credit institutions / investment firms providing portfolio management)
- SFDR 2.0: REMOVED from scope

Marketing/Naming Restrictions:
- SFDR 1.0: Limited restrictions via ESMA fund names guidelines (separate instrument)
- SFDR 2.0: Explicit statutory restrictions; non-categorized products cannot use sustainability terminology in names or marketing

Taxonomy Reporting Trigger:
- SFDR 1.0: Always required for Article 8/9 regardless of strategy
- SFDR 2.0: Only when relying on Taxonomy for category qualification (15% deemed compliance pathway)

Maximum Harmonization:
- SFDR 1.0: Minimum harmonization (member states could add requirements)
- SFDR 2.0: Maximum harmonization (member states CANNOT add requirements)
