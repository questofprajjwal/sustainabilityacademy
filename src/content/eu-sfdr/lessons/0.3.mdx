SFDR's implementation unfolded in stages over several years, reflecting the complexity of developing detailed technical standards and giving the industry time to build the necessary data infrastructure. Understanding this timeline is important for contextualising the current obligations and knowing what has changed since the regulation first entered into force.

## The Legislative Journey

**27 November 2019** — Regulation (EU) 2019/2088 (SFDR) was adopted by the European Parliament and the Council. It entered into force on 29 December 2019.

**10 March 2021 — Level 1 Application Date**

The core provisions of SFDR (Articles 3–7 and 10–12) became applicable on 10 March 2021. From this date, financial market participants were required to:
- Publish sustainability risk integration policies on their websites (Article 3)
- Publish PAI consideration statements — or explain why they do not consider PAI (Article 4 comply-or-explain)
- Align remuneration policies with sustainability risk integration (Article 5)
- Include sustainability risk disclosures in pre-contractual documents for all financial products (Article 6)
- Include Article 8 or Article 9 disclosures in pre-contractual documents where applicable (Articles 8–9)
- Maintain website disclosures for Article 8 and 9 products (Article 10)
- Include sustainability information in periodic reports (Article 11)

<HighlightBox>
The March 2021 application date is referred to as "Level 1" because the detailed technical standards (the Regulatory Technical Standards, or RTS) were not yet in force. Firms had to comply with the high-level obligations of the regulation but could use their own formats for disclosures. This created significant inconsistency across the market.
</HighlightBox>

## Development of the Regulatory Technical Standards

The three European Supervisory Authorities — the European Banking Authority (EBA), the European Insurance and Occupational Pensions Authority (EIOPA), and the European Securities and Markets Authority (ESMA), collectively the ESAs — were mandated by SFDR to develop detailed RTS specifying the content, methodologies, and presentation of disclosures.

**March 2021** — The ESAs submitted their final draft RTS to the European Commission.

**6 April 2022** — The European Commission adopted **Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2022/1288** — the SFDR RTS. This was published in the Official Journal of the European Union on 25 July 2022 (L 196).

**27 December 2022** — A **corrigendum** to the RTS was published (OJ L 332), correcting technical errors in the original text. This is the document formally designated CELEX:32022R1288R(01):EN:TXT.

## Level 2 Application — 1 January 2023

The RTS became applicable on **1 January 2023**. From this date:
- Pre-contractual and periodic disclosures for Article 8 and Article 9 products must use the standardised templates set out in the RTS Annexes
- The detailed PAI indicator framework (Annex I of the RTS) is mandatory for those considering PAI
- Website disclosures must follow the format requirements of the RTS

**30 June 2023 — First PAI Statement Deadline**

Firms that had elected to consider PAI (whether mandatorily, because they exceeded the 500-employee threshold, or voluntarily) were required to publish their **first PAI statement** covering the reference period 1 January 2022 to 31 December 2022 by **30 June 2023**.

<ExampleBox>
**Example — Timeline in practice:**

An asset manager with 600 employees managing several UCITS funds:

- **March 2021**: Published an entity-level sustainability risk integration policy on its website (Article 3), confirmed it would consider PAI (Article 4 — mandatory, as it exceeded 500 employees), and updated pre-contractual documents for all funds with sustainability risk disclosures (Article 6). Classified its two ESG-screened funds as Article 8.

- **January 2023**: Updated all Article 8 fund pre-contractual documents to use the RTS standardised templates (Annexes II/III). Began measuring PAI indicators using the RTS Annex I methodology.

- **June 2023**: Published its first formal PAI statement covering the 2022 reference period, including all 14 mandatory indicators for investments in investee companies, plus the 2 mandatory sovereign indicators.

- **June 2024**: Published its second PAI statement covering the 2023 reference period, now required to include a year-on-year comparison with the prior period.
</ExampleBox>

## The 2023 Amendment: Delegated Regulation 2023/363

**20 February 2023** — The European Commission adopted **Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2023/363**, which amended the original RTS. This amendment:
- Made technical corrections to certain PAI indicator formulas
- Clarified disclosure requirements for financial products with underlying investment options (multi-option products, or MOPs)
- Adjusted certain template requirements

The 2023 amendment entered into force on **20 March 2023** and applied from the same date.

## The SFDR Review Process

Recognising that the Article 6/8/9 classification system had evolved into a de facto product labelling system — despite not being designed as one — the European Commission announced a **comprehensive review of SFDR** in autumn 2023.

The review published a public consultation in September 2023 seeking views on:
- Whether to introduce a formal product categorisation system with binding criteria
- How to improve the definition of sustainable investment to ensure consistency
- Whether to simplify and streamline disclosure templates
- How to address the interaction between SFDR and other sustainable finance legislation

<HighlightBox>
The SFDR review reflects a broader recognition that disclosure-based approaches alone are insufficient to prevent greenwashing. Regulators are considering whether minimum substantive criteria should accompany disclosure requirements for products classified at the higher end of the sustainability spectrum (what would become Article 8 and Article 9 equivalents in a revised framework).
</HighlightBox>

## Ongoing Regulatory Guidance

In parallel with formal legislative developments, supervisory guidance has been issued continuously:

- **ESMA Consolidated SFDR Q&As** — updated periodically, most recently in November 2025, covering scope, PAI calculations, product disclosures, taxonomy alignment, and multi-option products
- **EC SFDR FAQ (April 2023)** — the European Commission's own clarificatory guidance on interpretation of the Level 1 text
- **National competent authority (NCA) guidance** — individual NCAs such as the AMF (France), BaFin (Germany), and CBI (Ireland) have issued national guidance that supplements the pan-EU framework

Understanding this timeline matters because it explains why the market currently has a mix of legacy disclosures (using pre-RTS formats) and updated disclosures (using the standardised RTS templates). It also explains why supervisory scrutiny of classification decisions has intensified since January 2023, when the detailed technical requirements gave regulators clearer benchmarks against which to assess product disclosures.
