IPCC AR6 Working Group I Press Release
"Climate Change 2021: The Physical Science Basis"
Released: August 9, 2021
Source: https://www.ipcc.ch/2021/08/09/ar6-wg1-20210809-pr/

MAIN HEADLINES

The IPCC released its Sixth Assessment Report (AR6) Working Group I contribution, titled "Climate Change 2021: The Physical Science Basis," approved by 195 member governments.

KEY FINDINGS

Unprecedented Changes:
Scientists observe climate changes in every region and across all climate systems. Many changes are "unprecedented in thousands, if not hundreds of thousands of years," with some alterations (like sea level rise) becoming irreversible over centuries or millennia.

Human Responsibility:
Human activities are responsible for approximately 1.1°C of warming since 1850-1900. The report states that "unless there are immediate, rapid and large-scale reductions in greenhouse gas emissions," limiting warming to 1.5°C or 2°C will become impossible.

Temperature Trajectory:
Over the next 20 years, global temperatures are expected to reach or exceed 1.5°C of warming. Stabilizing climate "will require strong, rapid, and sustained reductions in greenhouse gas emissions, and reaching net zero CO2 emissions."

REGIONAL IMPACTS

The report projects increasing climate changes across all regions:
- Heat waves becoming more frequent with 1.5°C warming
- Water cycle intensification causing increased rainfall, flooding, and drought
- Coastal threats with sea level rise continuing through the 21st century; 100-year extreme events potentially occurring annually by century's end
- Arctic warming exceeding global averages by more than double
- Ocean changes including warming, acidification, and reduced oxygen levels
