IPCC AR6 Working Group II Press Release
"Climate Change 2022: Impacts, Adaptation and Vulnerability"
Released: February 28, 2022
Source: https://www.ipcc.ch/2022/02/28/pr-wgii-ar6/

MAIN FINDING:
Human-induced climate change creates widespread disruption in nature and threatens billions globally, with vulnerable populations experiencing the greatest hardship.

CRITICAL QUOTES:

IPCC Chair Hoesung Lee stated: "This report is a dire warning about the consequences of inaction."

He added that climate change represents "a grave and mounting threat to our wellbeing and a healthy planet."

Working Group II Co-Chair Hans-Otto Pörtner emphasized: "Healthy ecosystems are more resilient to climate change and provide life-critical services."

Co-Chair Debra Roberts noted: "Our assessment clearly shows that tackling all these different challenges involves everyone."

KEY FINDINGS:

- Global warming of 1.5°C (2.7°F) will produce unavoidable climate hazards over the next two decades
- Exceeding this threshold even temporarily creates irreversible impacts
- Mass mortalities occurring in trees, corals, and other species from heat, drought, and flooding
- Millions face acute food and water insecurity across Africa, Asia, and other regions
- Adaptation progress remains uneven, with largest gaps among lower-income populations
- Conserving 30-50% of Earth's land, freshwater, and ocean habitats supports carbon storage
