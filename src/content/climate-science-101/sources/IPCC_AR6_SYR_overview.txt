IPCC AR6 Synthesis Report: Climate Change 2023
Overview and Key Findings
Source: https://www.ipcc.ch/report/sixth-assessment-report-cycle/
Crawled: March 2026

REPORT OVERVIEW:
The IPCC finalized the Sixth Assessment Report's Synthesis Report during the Panel's 58th Session held in Interlaken, Switzerland (March 13-19, 2023).

REPORT FOUNDATION:
The Synthesis Report synthesizes three Working Group Assessment Reports and three Special Reports:

Working Groups:
- WGI: The Physical Science Basis (2021)
- WGII: Impacts, Adaptation and Vulnerability (2022)
- WGIII: Mitigation of Climate Change (2022)

Special Reports:
- Global Warming of 1.5°C (SR1.5, 2018)
- Climate Change and Land (SRCCL, 2019)
- The Ocean and Cryosphere in a Changing Climate (SROCC, 2019)

REPORT STRUCTURE:
The approved outline consists of three main timeframe-based sections:
1. "Current Status and Trends" - historical and present conditions
2. "Long-term Climate and Development Futures" - projections to 2100 and beyond
3. "Near-term Responses in a Changing Climate" - policy timeframes through 2030-2040

AR6 SYNTHESIS REPORT KEY HEADLINES (from WGI Press Release, August 2021):

- Human activities are responsible for approximately 1.1°C of warming since 1850-1900
- Many changes are "unprecedented in thousands, if not hundreds of thousands of years"
- "Unless there are immediate, rapid and large-scale reductions in greenhouse gas emissions," limiting warming to 1.5°C or 2°C will become impossible
- Over the next 20 years, global temperatures are expected to reach or exceed 1.5°C of warming
- Sea level rise will continue through the 21st century; 100-year extreme events potentially occurring annually by century's end
- Arctic warming exceeds global averages by more than double

KEY CROSS-REPORT THEMES:

From WGII (Impacts):
- Global warming of 1.5°C will produce unavoidable climate hazards over the next two decades
- Exceeding this threshold even temporarily creates irreversible impacts
- Mass mortalities occurring in trees, corals, and other species from heat, drought, and flooding
- Adaptation progress remains uneven, with largest gaps among lower-income populations
- Conserving 30-50% of Earth's land, freshwater, and ocean habitats supports carbon storage

From WGIII (Mitigation):
- Global greenhouse gas emissions in 2010-2019 reached their highest levels ever
- Since 2010, solar and wind energy costs, plus battery technology, have dropped up to 85%
- For 1.5°C limit: emissions must peak before 2025, reduce by 43% by 2030, reach net-zero CO2 in early 2050s
- For 2°C limit: emissions peak before 2025, reduce by 25% by 2030, net-zero CO2 in early 2070s
- Financial flows are 3-6 times below needed 2030 levels
