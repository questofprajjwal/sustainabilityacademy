<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-800 mb-4">AFOLU Risk Tool: Natural Risks, Climate Change & Final Buffer</h2>

<HighlightBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">This lesson covers</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">Natural risks, fire, pest outbreaks, extreme weather, geological events, and sea-level rise, form the third and often most variable component of the non-permanence risk score. Version 4.2 added a new <strong>climate change amplification factor</strong>, reflecting IPCC AR6 projections that many natural risks will intensify over the 100-year assessment horizon.</p>
</HighlightBox>

<AnalogyBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-blue-800">Analogy: Home Insurance Premium Calculation</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-blue-900">Natural risk scoring works like an insurer calculating your home insurance premium. They ask: how often does this area flood? (Likelihood) How much would a flood damage? (Significance) Do you have flood barriers installed? (Mitigation). High likelihood x high damage x no mitigation = high premium. In VM0042, the "premium" is the buffer percentage you contribute before any VCU can be sold.</p>
</AnalogyBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Climate Change Amplification (CIDs), New in v4.2</h3>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">Version 4.2 added a <strong>Climatic Impact Driver (CID)</strong> amplification factor based on IPCC AR6. Risks affected by climate change (fire, pest/disease, extreme weather) are multiplied by a factor between <strong>1.0 and 1.4</strong>, reflecting projections that these risks will intensify over the 100-year assessment horizon.</p>

<EquationBreakdown
  title="Total Natural Risk with Climate Factor"
  result="Total Natural Risk | Total Natural Risk | The combined natural risk score after applying climate amplification and adding all components | blue"
  inputs="NR-c | Climate-Sensitive Risks | Sum of sub-totals for fire, pest/disease, and extreme weather, multiplied by Climate Change Factor (1.0-1.4) | green ;; NR-nc | Non-Climate Risks | Sum of sub-totals for geological and other non-climate-sensitive risks, multiplied by 1.0 | amber ;; SLR | Sea-Level Rise | Additional risk score for coastal projects only (zero for inland projects) | violet"
  operator="+"
/>

<div class="bg-amber-50 border border-amber-200 rounded p-4 mb-6 text-sm">
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">Adaptive Capacity Reduction</p>
  <p class="mt-1 text-amber-900">If the project meets at least <strong>5 of 7 adaptive capacity criteria</strong>, the climate change amplification fraction is reduced by 40%. The seven criteria (Table 12) assess whether the project proponent has embedded climate adaptation into its governance:</p>
  <ol class="list-decimal ml-6 mt-2 space-y-1 text-amber-900">
    <li><strong>Variety</strong>, Range of policy options; multi-sector governance</li>
    <li><strong>Learning capacity</strong>, Incorporates lessons from past climate events</li>
    <li><strong>Room for change</strong>, Uses climate information at different scales (early warning, projections)</li>
    <li><strong>Leadership</strong>, Long-term vision; proactive or collaborative climate action</li>
    <li><strong>Resources</strong>, Environmental science expertise; financial resources for adaptation</li>
    <li><strong>Fair governance</strong>, Accountability procedures and impact indicators</li>
    <li><strong>Innovation</strong>, Novel or traditional solutions to reduce climate threat</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<ExampleBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">Climate Factor Application: Kenya Project</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-sm">Climate-affected risks: Fire (sub-total = 2.0), Pest/Disease (3.0), Extreme Weather (2.5) - NR-c sum = 7.5</p>
  <p class="text-sm">Non-climate risk: Geological (sub-total = 0.0) - NR-nc = 0</p>
  <p class="text-sm mt-1">IPCC AR6 CID analysis for East Africa projects suggests temperature and drought intensification - Climate Change Factor = <strong>1.25</strong></p>
  <p class="text-sm mt-1">Project meets 5/7 adaptive capacity criteria (has learning capacity, resources, room for change, leadership, and governance) - adaptive capacity reduces amplification fraction by 40%:</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-sm bg-slate-100 p-2 rounded mt-2">NR-c = 7.5 x 1.25 = 9.375; but with 40% adaptive capacity reduction of the amplification:</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-sm bg-slate-100 p-2 rounded mt-1">Adjusted factor = 1.0 + (0.25 x 0.60) = 1.15 - NR-c = 7.5 x 1.15 = <strong>8.63</strong></p>
  <p class="text-sm font-bold text-amber-800 mt-2">Total Natural Risk = 8.63 + 0 (SLR not applicable) = <strong>8.63</strong></p>
</ExampleBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Sea-Level Rise (SLR) Risk</h3>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">Applies only to coastal projects (wetland restoration, mangrove ALM, etc.). Uses a separate table based on the overall SLR impact level (High/Major/Minor/Low/Insignificant) rather than historical frequency.</p>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">SLR adaptation measures reduce the score:</p>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-4 text-sm">
  <tr><th>Adaptation Measure</th><th>Multiplier</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Ecosystem-based adaptation (EbA): mangrove restoration, inland migration buffers</td><td>0.50</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Land use planning, participation, livelihood support</td><td>0.50</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Protection barriers: bioengineering, breakwaters</td><td>0.60</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Two or more measures combined</td><td>0.25</td></tr>
  <tr><td>None</td><td>1.00</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Calculating the Final Buffer Contribution</h3>

<EquationBreakdown
  title="Overall Non-Permanence Risk Rating"
  result="Total Risk | Total Risk Rating | The sum of all three risk categories, rounded up to the nearest whole percent to become the buffer contribution rate | blue"
  inputs="Internal | Internal Risk | Sum of Project Management, Financial Viability, Opportunity Cost, and Project Longevity scores | green ;; External | External Risk | Sum of Land Tenure, Stakeholder Engagement, and Political Risk scores | amber ;; Natural | Natural Risk | Sum of all natural risk sub-totals with climate amplification applied | violet"
  operator="+"
/>

<p class="text-sm text-slate-600 mb-2">Buffer credits = Net VCUs earned x Buffer rate</p>
<p class="text-sm text-slate-600 mb-4">Marketable VCUs = Net VCUs earned - Buffer credits</p>

<ExampleBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">Complete Buffer Calculation: Kenya Project</p>
  <ResponsiveTable>
<table class="text-sm mt-2">
    <tr><th>Category</th><th>Score</th></tr>
    <tr><td>Internal Risk (PM + FV + OC + PL)</td><td>17</td></tr>
    <tr><td>External Risk (LT + SE + PC)</td><td>2</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Natural Risk (NR-c x 1.15 + NR-nc)</td><td>8.63</td></tr>
    <tr class="font-bold"><td>Total Risk Rating</td><td>27.63 - rounded UP to <strong>28%</strong></td></tr>
  </table>
</ResponsiveTable>
  <p class="mt-3 text-sm">In the project's first verification period, 10,000 tCO₂e of net SOC removals are quantified:</p>
  <ul class="list-disc ml-6 text-sm mt-1 space-y-1">
    <li>Buffer credits deposited: 10,000 x 0.28 = <strong>2,800 VCUs</strong> (withheld; not for sale)</li>
    <li>Marketable VCUs: 10,000 - 2,800 = <strong>7,200 VCUs</strong> (can be sold)</li>
    <li>At $25/VCU: <strong>$180,000</strong> net revenue in first period</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="text-sm mt-2 text-amber-800">The 2,800 buffer VCUs sit in the AFOLU Pooled Buffer Account for the life of the project. Every 5 years, 15% of the accumulated buffer is released back to the project if no reversal events have occurred.</p>
</ExampleBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">The Buffer Release Mechanism</h3>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">Buffer credits are not locked away forever. The VCS Program releases them over time if no reversal events occur:</p>
<ul class="list-disc ml-6 text-sm space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li>Every 5 years of monitoring without a reversal event: <strong>15% of the project's cumulative buffer</strong> is released back to the project proponent as marketable VCUs</li>
  <li>If a reversal event occurs (fire destroys carbon stocks, farmer abandons practice), Verra cancels buffer credits from the pool to compensate</li>
  <li>The pooled account means that all AFOLU projects share the insurance, a small number of reversals are absorbed without any individual project bearing the full loss</li>
</ul>

<div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded p-4 text-sm">
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Summary: The Complete Risk Calculation Flow</p>
  <ol class="list-decimal ml-6 mt-2 space-y-2 text-green-900">
    <li>Score each of 4 Internal sub-categories (PM + FV + OC + PL); check for individual FAIL conditions</li>
    <li>Score each of 3 External sub-categories (LT + SE + PC); check for individual FAIL conditions</li>
    <li>Score each natural risk factor using the LS x M formula (Table 10 + mitigation)</li>
    <li>Apply the CID climate change amplification factor (1.0-1.4) to climate-sensitive risks; reduce by 40% if ≥5 adaptive capacity criteria are met</li>
    <li>Add SLR score for coastal projects</li>
    <li>Verify: Natural risk &le; 35, Internal risk &le; 35, External risk &le; 20, and no individual FAIL conditions</li>
    <li>Sum all three categories - round UP to nearest whole percent = buffer rate; apply 12% minimum floor</li>
    <li>Verify overall risk &le; 60 (otherwise project FAILS entirely)</li>
    <li>Buffer credits = net SOC VCUs x buffer rate; marketable VCUs = net VCUs - buffer credits</li>
  </ol>
</div>
