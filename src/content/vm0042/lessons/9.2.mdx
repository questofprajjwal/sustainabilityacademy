<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-800 mb-4">AFOLU Risk Tool: Natural Risks, Climate Change & Final Buffer</h2>

<HighlightBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">This lesson covers</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">Natural risks — fire, pest outbreaks, extreme weather, geological events, and sea-level rise — form the third and often most variable component of the non-permanence risk score. Version 4.2 added a new <strong>climate change amplification factor</strong>, reflecting IPCC AR6 projections that many natural risks will intensify over the 100-year assessment horizon.</p>
</HighlightBox>

<AnalogyBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-blue-800">🌪️ Analogy: Home Insurance Premium Calculation</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-blue-900">Natural risk scoring works like an insurer calculating your home insurance premium. They ask: how often does this area flood? (Likelihood) How much would a flood damage? (Significance) Do you have flood barriers installed? (Mitigation). High likelihood × high damage × no mitigation = high premium. In VM0042, the "premium" is the buffer percentage you contribute before any VCU can be sold.</p>
</AnalogyBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">How Natural Risk Scoring Works</h3>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">For each natural risk factor, calculate a <strong>sub-total risk score</strong> using three inputs:</p>

<FormulaBox>
  <p class="font-bold text-green-300 mb-2">Sub-total Natural Risk per Factor</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-xl text-yellow-200">Sub-total = LS × M</p>
  <div class="grid grid-cols-1 gap-2 mt-3 text-sm text-slate-300">
    <div><strong>LS</strong> = score from Table 10, based on: Likelihood (historical frequency over 100 years) × Significance (% of carbon stocks lost per event)</div>
    <div><strong>M</strong> = mitigation factor: 1.0 (no mitigation), 0.50 (one mitigation), 0.25 (both mitigations)</div>
  </div>
</FormulaBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Table 10: Historic Natural Risk Scores (LS Values)</h3>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-6 text-sm">
  <tr><th>Significance (loss per event)</th><th>&gt;once/10yr</th><th>10–&lt;25yr</th><th>25–&lt;50yr</th><th>50–&lt;100yr</th><th>Once per 100yr or less / Not applicable</th></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Catastrophic (≥70%)</strong></td><td class="bg-red-200 text-red-900 font-bold">FAIL</td><td>30</td><td>20</td><td>5</td><td>4</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Devastating (50% to &lt;70%)</strong></td><td>30</td><td>20</td><td>5</td><td>4</td><td>3</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Major (25% to &lt;50%)</strong></td><td>20</td><td>5</td><td>4</td><td>3</td><td>2</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Minor (5% to &lt;25%)</strong></td><td>5</td><td>4</td><td>3</td><td>2</td><td>1</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Insignificant (&lt;5%) or transient</strong></td><td>4</td><td>3</td><td>2</td><td>1</td><td>1</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Not applicable</strong></td><td>0</td><td>0</td><td>0</td><td>0</td><td>0</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>
<p class="text-sm text-slate-600 mb-4"><em>Transient = full recovery within 10 years (e.g., drought that briefly reduces SOC but soil recovers)</em></p>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Mitigation Scores (M)</h3>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-6 text-sm">
  <tr><th>Mitigation Condition</th><th>M Value</th><th>Effect</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Prevention measures in place for this risk factor</td><td>0.50</td><td>Risk score halved</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Demonstrated history of effectively containing this risk</td><td>0.50</td><td>Risk score halved</td></tr>
  <tr><td>BOTH prevention measures AND demonstrated containment history</td><td>0.25</td><td>Risk score reduced to 25%</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Neither</td><td>1.00</td><td>No reduction</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>
<div class="bg-blue-50 border border-blue-200 rounded p-3 mb-6 text-sm">
  <p class="font-semibold text-blue-800">Mitigation Examples by Risk Factor</p>
  <ul class="mt-1 space-y-1 text-blue-900">
    <li><strong>Fire:</strong> Fuel removal, fire breaks, fire towers, firefighting equipment</li>
    <li><strong>Pest/Disease:</strong> Biodiverse planting, pest-resistant species, co-planting inhibiting vegetation</li>
    <li><strong>Extreme Weather:</strong> Frost-tolerant species, riparian flood zones, species tolerant of waterlogging</li>
  </ul>
</div>

  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📐 Natural Risk Calculation: Semi-Arid Kenya Project</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-sm">For the Rift Valley Kenya project, assess fire, pest/disease, and extreme weather:</p>

    <tr><td>Pest/Disease (PD)</td><td>Once per 30 years (25–50yr band)</td><td>Minor (5–15% loss)</td><td>3</td><td>No active prevention</td><td>1.00</td><td>3.0</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Extreme Weather (W)</td><td>Once per 12 years (10–25yr band)</td><td>Major (30% loss in drought)</td><td>5</td><td>Drought-tolerant species, soil moisture monitoring</td><td>0.50</td><td>2.5</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Geological (G)</td><td>N/A (no seismic/volcanic history)</td><td>Not applicable</td><td>0</td><td>N/A</td><td>1.00</td><td>0</td></tr>
  </table>
</ResponsiveTable>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Climate Change Amplification (CIDs) — New in v4.2</h3>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">Version 4.2 added a <strong>Climatic Impact Driver (CID)</strong> amplification factor based on IPCC AR6. Risks affected by climate change (fire, pest/disease, extreme weather) are multiplied by a factor between <strong>1.0 and 1.4</strong>, reflecting projections that these risks will intensify over the 100-year assessment horizon.</p>

<FormulaBox>
  <p class="font-bold text-green-300 mb-2">Total Natural Risk with Climate Factor</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-base text-yellow-200">Total Natural Risk = NR-c + NR-nc + SLR</p>
  <div class="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-300">
    <p><strong>NR-c</strong> = Sum of sub-totals for climate-sensitive risks × Climate Change Factor (1.0–1.4)</p>
    <p class="mt-1"><strong>NR-nc</strong> = Sum of sub-totals for non-climate-sensitive risks × 1.0</p>
    <p class="mt-1"><strong>SLR</strong> = Sea-level rise risk (coastal projects only)</p>
  </div>
</FormulaBox>

<div class="bg-amber-50 border border-amber-200 rounded p-4 mb-6 text-sm">
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">Adaptive Capacity Reduction</p>
  <p class="mt-1 text-amber-900">If the project meets at least <strong>5 of 7 adaptive capacity criteria</strong>, the climate change amplification fraction is reduced by 40%. The seven criteria (Table 12) assess whether the project proponent has embedded climate adaptation into its governance:</p>
  <ol class="list-decimal ml-6 mt-2 space-y-1 text-amber-900">
    <li><strong>Variety</strong> — Range of policy options; multi-sector governance</li>
    <li><strong>Learning capacity</strong> — Incorporates lessons from past climate events</li>
    <li><strong>Room for change</strong> — Uses climate information at different scales (early warning, projections)</li>
    <li><strong>Leadership</strong> — Long-term vision; proactive or collaborative climate action</li>
    <li><strong>Resources</strong> — Environmental science expertise; financial resources for adaptation</li>
    <li><strong>Fair governance</strong> — Accountability procedures and impact indicators</li>
    <li><strong>Innovation</strong> — Novel or traditional solutions to reduce climate threat</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<ExampleBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📐 Climate Factor Application: Kenya Project</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-sm">Climate-affected risks: Fire (sub-total = 2.0), Pest/Disease (3.0), Extreme Weather (2.5) → NR-c sum = 7.5</p>
  <p class="text-sm">Non-climate risk: Geological (sub-total = 0.0) → NR-nc = 0</p>
  <p class="text-sm mt-1">IPCC AR6 CID analysis for East Africa projects suggests temperature and drought intensification → Climate Change Factor = <strong>1.25</strong></p>
  <p class="text-sm mt-1">Project meets 5/7 adaptive capacity criteria (has learning capacity, resources, room for change, leadership, and governance) → adaptive capacity reduces amplification fraction by 40%:</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-sm bg-slate-100 p-2 rounded mt-2">NR-c = 7.5 × 1.25 = 9.375; but with 40% adaptive capacity reduction of the amplification:</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-sm bg-slate-100 p-2 rounded mt-1">Adjusted factor = 1.0 + (0.25 × 0.60) = 1.15 → NR-c = 7.5 × 1.15 = <strong>8.63</strong></p>
  <p class="text-sm font-bold text-amber-800 mt-2">Total Natural Risk = 8.63 + 0 (SLR not applicable) = <strong>8.63</strong></p>
</ExampleBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Sea-Level Rise (SLR) Risk</h3>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">Applies only to coastal projects (wetland restoration, mangrove ALM, etc.). Uses a separate table based on the overall SLR impact level (High/Major/Minor/Low/Insignificant) rather than historical frequency.</p>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">SLR adaptation measures reduce the score:</p>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-4 text-sm">
  <tr><th>Adaptation Measure</th><th>Multiplier</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Ecosystem-based adaptation (EbA): mangrove restoration, inland migration buffers</td><td>0.50</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Land use planning, participation, livelihood support</td><td>0.50</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Protection barriers: bioengineering, breakwaters</td><td>0.60</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Two or more measures combined</td><td>0.25</td></tr>
  <tr><td>None</td><td>1.00</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Calculating the Final Buffer Contribution</h3>
<FormulaBox>
  <p class="font-bold text-green-300 mb-2">Overall Non-Permanence Risk Rating</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-xl text-yellow-200">Total Risk = Internal + External + Natural</p>
  <p class="text-slate-300 text-sm mt-2">Round UP to nearest whole percent → Buffer contribution rate</p>
  <p class="text-slate-300 text-sm mt-1">Buffer credits = Net VCUs earned × Buffer rate</p>
  <p class="text-slate-300 text-sm mt-1">Marketable VCUs = Net VCUs earned − Buffer credits</p>
</FormulaBox>

<ExampleBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📐 Complete Buffer Calculation: Kenya Project</p>
  <ResponsiveTable>
<table class="text-sm mt-2">
    <tr><th>Category</th><th>Score</th></tr>
    <tr><td>Internal Risk (PM + FV + OC + PL)</td><td>17</td></tr>
    <tr><td>External Risk (LT + SE + PC)</td><td>2</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Natural Risk (NR-c × 1.15 + NR-nc)</td><td>8.63</td></tr>
    <tr class="font-bold"><td>Total Risk Rating</td><td>27.63 → rounded UP to <strong>28%</strong></td></tr>
  </table>
</ResponsiveTable>
  <p class="mt-3 text-sm">In the project's first verification period, 10,000 tCO₂e of net SOC removals are quantified:</p>
  <ul class="list-disc ml-6 text-sm mt-1 space-y-1">
    <li>Buffer credits deposited: 10,000 × 0.28 = <strong>2,800 VCUs</strong> (withheld; not for sale)</li>
    <li>Marketable VCUs: 10,000 − 2,800 = <strong>7,200 VCUs</strong> (can be sold)</li>
    <li>At $25/VCU: <strong>$180,000</strong> net revenue in first period</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="text-sm mt-2 text-amber-800">The 2,800 buffer VCUs sit in the AFOLU Pooled Buffer Account for the life of the project. Every 5 years, 15% of the accumulated buffer is released back to the project if no reversal events have occurred.</p>
</ExampleBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">The Buffer Release Mechanism</h3>
<p class="mb-3 text-sm">Buffer credits are not locked away forever. The VCS Program releases them over time if no reversal events occur:</p>
<ul class="list-disc ml-6 text-sm space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li>Every 5 years of monitoring without a reversal event: <strong>15% of the project's cumulative buffer</strong> is released back to the project proponent as marketable VCUs</li>
  <li>If a reversal event occurs (fire destroys carbon stocks, farmer abandons practice), Verra cancels buffer credits from the pool to compensate</li>
  <li>The pooled account means that all AFOLU projects share the insurance — a small number of reversals are absorbed without any individual project bearing the full loss</li>
</ul>

  <p class="text-red-900 text-xs mt-2"><strong>Critical for project economics:</strong> The 12% minimum floor means no matter how low your calculated score, at least 12% of all net SOC VCUs are withheld. A project calculating 6% buffer still loses 12% of its credits — project financial models must account for this.</p>
</div>

<div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded p-4 text-sm">
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Summary: The Complete Risk Calculation Flow</p>
  <ol class="list-decimal ml-6 mt-2 space-y-2 text-green-900">
    <li>Score each of 4 Internal sub-categories (PM + FV + OC + PL); check for individual FAIL conditions</li>
    <li>Score each of 3 External sub-categories (LT + SE + PC); check for individual FAIL conditions</li>
    <li>Score each natural risk factor using the LS × M formula (Table 10 + mitigation)</li>
    <li>Apply the CID climate change amplification factor (1.0–1.4) to climate-sensitive risks; reduce by 40% if ≥5 adaptive capacity criteria are met</li>
    <li>Add SLR score for coastal projects</li>
    <li>Verify: Natural risk ≤ 35, Internal risk ≤ 35, External risk ≤ 20, and no individual FAIL conditions</li>
    <li>Sum all three categories → round UP to nearest whole percent = buffer rate; apply 12% minimum floor</li>
    <li>Verify overall risk ≤ 60 (otherwise project FAILS entirely)</li>
    <li>Buffer credits = net SOC VCUs × buffer rate; marketable VCUs = net VCUs − buffer credits</li>
  </ol>
</div>
