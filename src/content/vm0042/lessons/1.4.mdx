<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-800 mb-4">The Five ALM Practice Categories in Depth</h2>
<HighlightBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">The "what" of a VM0042 project</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">Every VM0042 project must implement at least one of these five practice categories. Understanding each category, including its mechanism, examples, and which GHG it primarily affects, is essential for project design.</p>
</HighlightBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-4 mb-2">Category (a): Fertilizer Management</h3>
<div class="bg-white border rounded p-4 mb-4 shadow-sm">
  <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 mb-2"><strong>Mechanism:</strong> Reducing nitrogen inputs decreases N₂O emissions from soil nitrification/denitrification. Adding organic amendments increases carbon inputs to soil, raising SOC.</p>
  <p class="text-sm font-medium mb-2">Example practices:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm text-gray-700 list-disc ml-4 space-y-1">
    <li>Reduce synthetic N fertilizer application rate by ≥5%</li>
    <li>Switch to slow-release or enhanced-efficiency fertilizers</li>
    <li>Replace synthetic N with compost or manure</li>
    <li>Use nitrification inhibitors</li>
  </ul>
  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>📍 Vietnam Rice:</strong> A cooperative reduces urea from 200 → 140 kg N/ha/yr (30% reduction). N₂O emissions drop by ~25%. They also add 2 t/ha composted rice straw, increasing SOC inputs.
  </ExampleBox>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-4 mb-2">Category (b): Water Management / Irrigation</h3>
<div class="bg-white border rounded p-4 mb-4 shadow-sm">
  <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 mb-2"><strong>Mechanism:</strong> Reducing flooding in rice paddies dramatically cuts CH₄ from anaerobic decomposition. Efficient irrigation can improve crop biomass returns to soil.</p>
  <p class="text-sm font-medium mb-2">Example practices:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm text-gray-700 list-disc ml-4 space-y-1">
    <li>Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD) in rice</li>
    <li>Switch from flood to drip/furrow irrigation</li>
    <li>Deficit irrigation strategies</li>
  </ul>
  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>📍 Philippines Rice:</strong> AWD, draining paddies mid-season instead of continuous flooding. CH₄ emissions drop by 30–50%. This is one of the highest-impact practices in VM0042.
  </ExampleBox>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-4 mb-2">Category (c): Tillage / Residue Management</h3>
<div class="bg-white border rounded p-4 mb-4 shadow-sm">
  <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 mb-2"><strong>Mechanism:</strong> Less tillage = less soil disturbance = less SOC oxidation. Residue retention = more organic matter input to soil.</p>
  <p class="text-sm font-medium mb-2">Example practices:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm text-gray-700 list-disc ml-4 space-y-1">
    <li>Switch from conventional to no-till or reduced till</li>
    <li>Retain crop residues on field instead of removing or burning</li>
    <li>Change from fall to spring tillage</li>
  </ul>
  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>📍 India Wheat:</strong> Punjab wheat farmers switch from burning rice stubble (massive air pollution) to "happy seeder" technology, planting wheat directly into standing rice residue. Residue remains on field, adding ~1.2 tC/ha/yr to SOC.
  </ExampleBox>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-4 mb-2">Category (d): Crop Planting & Harvesting</h3>
<div class="bg-white border rounded p-4 mb-4 shadow-sm">
  <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 mb-2"><strong>Mechanism:</strong> More continuous ground cover and root biomass = more carbon inputs. Agroforestry adds woody biomass carbon (tracked separately).</p>
  <p class="text-sm font-medium mb-2">Example practices:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm text-gray-700 list-disc ml-4 space-y-1">
    <li>Planting cover crops in fallow periods</li>
    <li>Agroforestry (trees integrated into cropland)</li>
    <li>Diversified crop rotations (adding legumes, perennial phases)</li>
    <li>Improving species selection in perennial grasslands</li>
  </ul>
  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>📍 US Corn Belt:</strong> Corn farmers plant <em>cereal rye</em> as winter cover crop after corn harvest. Rye grows through winter, is terminated in spring, and its biomass decomposes into the soil, depositing approximately 0.5–1.0 tC/ha/yr additional carbon.
  </ExampleBox>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-4 mb-2">Category (e): Grazing Practices</h3>
<div class="bg-white border rounded p-4 mb-4 shadow-sm">
  <p class="text-sm text-gray-600 mb-2"><strong>Mechanism:</strong> Managed grazing allows grass recovery and deeper root growth, increasing SOC. Reduced stocking can lower enteric fermentation (CH₄) and manure (N₂O + CH₄) emissions.</p>
  <p class="text-sm font-medium mb-2">Example practices:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm text-gray-700 list-disc ml-4 space-y-1">
    <li>Rotational grazing / adaptive multi-paddock grazing</li>
    <li>Reducing stocking density on overgrazed land</li>
    <li>Replacing continuous grazing with seasonal rest periods</li>
  </ul>
  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>📍 Patagonia, Argentina:</strong> A cattle ranch shifts from continuous grazing to planned rotational grazing (60–90 day rest periods). Grass root depth increases from 10 → 30 cm, dramatically increasing belowground carbon inputs. SOC gains: 0.8–1.5 tC/ha/yr.
  </ExampleBox>
</div>

<AnalogyBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-blue-800">🎸 Analogy: A Band vs. a Solo Act</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-blue-900">A single practice category is like one musician. Stacking multiple categories is like forming a band, the combined effect is far greater than each individual contribution. Conservation Agriculture (no-till + cover crops + residue retention) is the "band" most often seen in VM0042 projects because it hits SOC, fossil fuel, and N₂O reduction simultaneously.</p>
</AnalogyBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Multiple Practices = "Stacked" Benefits</h3>
<p class="mb-4">Projects can combine multiple practice categories. Common "stacks":</p>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-6">
  <tr><th>Practice Stack</th><th>Categories</th><th>Combined GHG Benefit</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Conservation Agriculture</td><td>(c) no-till + (d) cover crops + (c) residue retention</td><td>↑ SOC, ↓ fossil fuel, ↓ N₂O</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Climate-Smart Rice</td><td>(b) AWD + (a) N management + (d) improved varieties</td><td>↓ CH₄, ↓ N₂O, ↑ SOC</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Regenerative Ranching</td><td>(e) rotational grazing + (d) improved grasses + (a) reduced synthetic N</td><td>↑ SOC, ↓ CH₄, ↓ N₂O</td></tr>
</table>
