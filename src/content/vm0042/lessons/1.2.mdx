<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-800 mb-4">Key Definitions You Must Know</h2>
<HighlightBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Why definitions matter</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">VM0042 uses specific technical terms with precise meanings. Using the wrong term — or misunderstanding one — can lead to costly errors in project design, calculations, and audits. This lesson demystifies the most important ones.</p>
</HighlightBox>

<AnalogyBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-blue-800">🏫 Analogy: Classrooms and Test Scores</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-blue-900">Imagine you are a teacher with 120 students across 4 classrooms. Each classroom is a <strong>quantification unit</strong> — you measure scores within each room. Classrooms 1 & 2 have similar demographics, so you group them into <strong>Stratum A</strong>. Classrooms 3 & 4 are different, forming <strong>Stratum B</strong>. In VM0042, "classrooms" are land parcels and "test scores" are SOC measurements.</p>
</AnalogyBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Core Definitions Table</h3>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-6">
  <tr><th>Term</th><th>Plain English</th><th>Real-World Example</th></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Quantification Unit (QU)</strong></td><td>A contiguous chunk of land where you measure and calculate carbon changes</td><td>A 200-ha block within a project with the same soil type and climate</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Stratum</strong></td><td>A group of QUs that share similar characteristics</td><td>All sandy-loam QUs in a semi-arid zone grouped together</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Baseline Control Site</strong></td><td>A nearby piece of land that keeps farming the old way — for comparison</td><td>A neighbouring farm that continues conventional tillage while the project farm switches to no-till</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Historical Look-back Period</strong></td><td>The time window you examine to figure out pre-project practices</td><td>5 years of cropping records before the project start date</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Schedule of Activities</strong></td><td>A documented table of what farming practices were done before and what will change</td><td>"Before: conventional tillage, no cover crops. After: no-till, winter rye cover crop"</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Improved ALM Practice</strong></td><td>Any change in farming that is expected to increase SOC or reduce emissions</td><td>Switching from flood irrigation to deficit irrigation</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Project Domain</strong></td><td>The set of conditions (crop type, soil, climate) where a model has been validated</td><td>DayCent model validated for maize on clay soils in tropical humid climate</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Annual (crop)</strong></td><td>A plant species that completes its life cycle within one year</td><td>Wheat, maize, rice, soybean</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Perennial (crop)</strong></td><td>A plant species whose life cycle extends across multiple years</td><td>Alfalfa, perennial grasses, sugarcane</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>Woody Perennials</strong></td><td>Trees and shrubs lasting more than two years</td><td>Coffee, cocoa, fruit trees in agroforestry systems</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Critical Distinction: QU vs. Stratum vs. Project Area</h3>
<div class="bg-gray-50 border rounded p-4 mb-6">
  <p class="font-medium text-gray-800 mb-2">Example: A 10,000 ha project in Kenya</p>
  <ul class="space-y-2 text-sm text-gray-700">
    <li>📦 <strong>Project area:</strong> All 10,000 ha — the total footprint of the project</li>
    <li>🔲 <strong>Quantification units:</strong> 500 individual farm fields (avg. 20 ha each) — each is measured separately</li>
    <li>🎨 <strong>Strata:</strong> Fields grouped by soil type: Stratum A = 200 fields on clay soils, Stratum B = 300 fields on sandy loam soils</li>
  </ul>
  <p class="mt-3 text-sm text-gray-500">Stratification allows more efficient sampling — you need fewer samples per stratum because within-stratum variability is lower.</p>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">The Schedule of Activities — What It Must Include</h3>
<p class="mb-3">For each quantification unit, the schedule documents ALM practices during the <strong>historical look-back period</strong> (minimum 3 years before project start). It must cover:</p>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-6">
  <tr><th>ALM Practice</th><th>Qualitative Info Needed</th><th>Quantitative Info Needed</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Crop planting</td><td>Crop type(s)</td><td>Planting/harvest dates, crop yield</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Nitrogen fertilizer</td><td>Synthetic? Manure? Compost?</td><td>Application rate (kg N/ha)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Tillage</td><td>Tilled? Residue removed?</td><td>Depth, frequency, % area disturbed</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Water management</td><td>Irrigated? Flooded?</td><td>Irrigation rate (mm/year)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Grazing</td><td>Grazed? Animal type?</td><td>Stocking rate, frequency</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>

<div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded p-4">
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Key Takeaway</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">The most important terms to master: <strong>Quantification Unit</strong> (where you measure), <strong>Stratum</strong> (how you group QUs), <strong>Baseline Control Site</strong> (the comparison land), and <strong>Schedule of Activities</strong> (the documented history of farming practices). These form the backbone of every VM0042 project.</p>
</div>
