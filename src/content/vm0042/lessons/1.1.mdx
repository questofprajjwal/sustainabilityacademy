<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-800 mb-4">What VM0042 v2.2 Actually Covers</h2>
<HighlightBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">In one sentence</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">VM0042 provides the rules for estimating and verifying <strong>greenhouse gas reductions and carbon removals</strong> that result from changing how agricultural land is managed — with a particular focus on increasing <strong>soil organic carbon (SOC)</strong>.</p>
</HighlightBox>
<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Full Name & Identity</h3>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-6">
  <tr><th>Field</th><th>Detail</th></tr>
  <tr><td>Full name</td><td>VM0042 Improved Agricultural Land Management</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Version</td><td>2.2 (October 2025)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Sectoral Scope</td><td>14 — Agriculture, Forestry and Other Land Use (AFOLU)</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Geography</td><td>Globally applicable</td></tr>
  <tr><td>GHGs tracked</td><td>CO₂, CH₄, N₂O</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Credit type</td><td>Reductions AND Removals</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Additionality method</td><td>Project Method</td></tr>
  <tr><td>Baseline method</td><td>Project Method</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>
<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">The Five ALM Practice Categories</h3>
<p class="mb-4">VM0042 covers any agricultural practice change that increases SOC or reduces net GHG emissions. These fall into five categories:</p>
<ResponsiveTable>
<table class="mb-6">
  <tr><th>Category</th><th>Examples</th><th>Primary GHG Benefit</th></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>(a) Fertilizer Management</strong></td><td>Reduce synthetic N, switch to slow-release, add compost/manure</td><td>↓ N₂O, ↑ SOC</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>(b) Water Management</strong></td><td>Drip irrigation, alternate wetting & drying in rice</td><td>↓ CH₄ (rice), ↑ SOC</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>(c) Tillage / Residue</strong></td><td>No-till, reduced tillage, residue retention (stop burning)</td><td>↑ SOC, ↓ N₂O</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>(d) Crop Planting & Harvest</strong></td><td>Cover crops, agroforestry, improved crop rotations</td><td>↑ SOC, carbon removal</td></tr>
  <tr><td><strong>(e) Grazing Practices</strong></td><td>Rotational grazing, reduce stocking density</td><td>↑ SOC, ↓ CH₄</td></tr>
</table>
</ResponsiveTable>
<ExampleBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📍 Real-World Example — Kenya</p>
  <p class="mt-2">A cooperative of 500 smallholder farmers in western Kenya has been practising continuous maize monocropping with conventional tillage. They want to switch to <strong>no-till + maize-bean intercropping + residue retention</strong>.</p>
  <p class="mt-2">This touches categories <strong>(c)</strong> (no-till, residue retention) and <strong>(d)</strong> (intercropping). VM0042 provides the rulebook for measuring how much additional carbon their soils store and how many credits they can earn.</p>
</ExampleBox>
<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Three Ways to Quantify Carbon</h3>
<p class="mb-4">VM0042 offers three quantification approaches depending on available resources and data:</p>
<div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-3 mb-6">
  <div class="bg-blue-50 border border-blue-200 rounded p-3 text-sm"><strong class="text-blue-800">Approach 1</strong><br />Measure & Model<br /><span class="text-gray-500 text-xs">Use a biogeochemical model calibrated with soil measurements</span></div>
  <div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded p-3 text-sm"><strong class="text-green-800">Approach 2</strong><br />Measure & Re-Measure<br /><span class="text-gray-500 text-xs">Directly measure SOC at project and control sites over time</span></div>
  <div class="bg-amber-50 border border-amber-200 rounded p-3 text-sm"><strong class="text-amber-800">Approach 3</strong><br />Default Factors<br /><span class="text-gray-500 text-xs">Use IPCC emission factors — simplest but less precise</span></div>
</div>
<div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded p-4">
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Key Takeaway</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">VM0042 is globally applicable to cropland and grassland projects. It covers 5 practice categories and 3 quantification approaches. The central question it answers: <em>"How many tonnes of CO₂e did this farming practice change avoid or remove?"</em></p>
</div>
