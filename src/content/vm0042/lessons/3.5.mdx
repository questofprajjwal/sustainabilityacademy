<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-800 mb-4">Net Reductions & Removals: The Master Equation</h2>
<HighlightBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Everything comes together here</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">This lesson assembles all the individual calculations from the previous lessons into a single net figure: <strong>Estimated Net Reductions and Removals (ERR<sub>NET</sub>)</strong>. This is the number that determines how many carbon credits the project earns.</p>
</HighlightBox>

<AnalogyBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-blue-800">🏦 Analogy: Your Net Salary</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-blue-900">ERR_NET is like your take-home pay. Your gross earnings are the total carbon removed and emissions reduced. Leakage is like tax withheld. Uncertainty is like an employer holding back a portion until you prove the work was done correctly. The buffer pool is the mandatory pension contribution. What ends up in your bank account (VCUs issued) is what's left after all deductions.</p>
</AnalogyBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">The Overall Structure</h3>
<FormulaBox>
  <p class="font-mono text-sm">ERR<sub>NET,t</sub> = ER<sub>NET,t</sub> + CR<sub>NET,t</sub></p>
  <br />
  <p class="font-mono text-sm">ER<sub>NET,t</sub> = ER<sub>t</sub> − LK<sub>ER,t</sub>     (Net Emission Reductions)</p>
  <p class="font-mono text-sm">CR<sub>NET,t</sub> = CR<sub>t</sub> − LK<sub>CR,t</sub>     (Net Carbon Dioxide Removals)</p>
  <br />
  <p class="text-gray-400 text-xs">Where: ER = emission reductions; CR = carbon removals; LK = leakage allocated proportionally</p>
</FormulaBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Emission Reductions (ER), Equation 37</h3>
<p class="text-sm mb-3">Emission reductions occur when GHG emissions are lower in the project than in the baseline. This includes all non-SOC GHG sources plus cases where SOC stocks are declining in the baseline:</p>
<FormulaBox>
  ER<sub>t</sub> = ΔGHG<sub>ff</sub> + ΔGHG<sub>lime</sub> + ΔCH₄<sub>ent</sub> + ΔCH₄<sub>md</sub> + ΔCH₄<sub>bb</sub> + (ΔCH₄<sub>soil</sub> × (1 − UNC)) + (ΔN₂O<sub>soil</sub> × (1 − UNC)) + ΔN₂O<sub>bb</sub> + SOC change component
</FormulaBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">Carbon Removals (CR), Equation 40</h3>
<p class="text-sm mb-3">Removals occur when the cumulative carbon stock in the project scenario increases <em>above the starting level</em>:</p>
<FormulaBox>
  CR<sub>t</sub> = MAX(0, ΔCO₂<sub>wp,t</sub>) − MAX(0, ΔCO₂<sub>bsl,t</sub>)
</FormulaBox>
<p class="text-sm mb-4 text-gray-600">In simple terms: removals = (project SOC gain above baseline) × uncertainty factor, when cumulative project SOC is increasing.</p>

<ExampleBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📐 Full Worked Example: 5,000 ha US Corn Belt No-Till Project (5 years)</p>

  <p class="font-medium mt-3 text-sm">Input Data:</p>
  <ResponsiveTable>
<table class="text-sm mt-2">
    <tr><th>Source</th><th>Baseline (total, 5yr)</th><th>Project (total, 5yr)</th><th>Reduction</th></tr>
    <tr><td>SOC change (CO₂)</td><td>+1,500 tCO₂ (slow SOC loss)</td><td>+33,000 tCO₂ (SOC gain)</td><td>See below*</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Fossil fuel</td><td>3,000 tCO₂</td><td>1,000 tCO₂</td><td class="text-green-700">2,000 tCO₂</td></tr>
    <tr><td>N₂O (N fertilizer)</td><td>3,750 tCO₂e</td><td>2,500 tCO₂e</td><td class="text-green-700">1,250 tCO₂e</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Leakage (all types)</td><td>—</td><td>—</td><td class="text-red-600">−0 (de minimis)</td></tr>
  </table>
</ResponsiveTable>

  <p class="text-sm mt-3">*SOC component:</p>
  <ul class="text-sm mt-1 space-y-1">
    <li>Project SOC cumulative change = +33,000 tCO₂ (soil is sequestering carbon)</li>
    <li>Baseline SOC cumulative change = +1,500 tCO₂ (small natural gain in baseline)</li>
    <li><strong>Net CO₂ removal = 33,000 − 1,500 = 31,500 tCO₂ removals</strong></li>
  </ul>

  <p class="font-medium mt-4 text-sm">Totals (before uncertainty deduction):</p>
  <ResponsiveTable>
<table class="text-sm mt-2">
    <tr><td><strong>Non-SOC emission reductions</strong></td><td class="text-green-700 font-bold">3,250 tCO₂e</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>SOC carbon removals</strong></td><td class="text-green-700 font-bold">31,500 tCO₂e</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Total ERR (gross)</strong></td><td class="text-green-800 font-bold text-base">34,750 tCO₂e</td></tr>
    <tr><td>Uncertainty deduction (assume 15% on SOC)</td><td class="text-red-600">−4,725 tCO₂e</td></tr>
    <tr><td><strong>Total ERR (net, before buffer)</strong></td><td class="text-green-800 font-bold text-base">30,025 tCO₂e</td></tr>
  </table>
</ResponsiveTable>
  <p class="text-xs text-amber-700 mt-2">Uncertainty and buffer discussed in Module 4.</p>
</ExampleBox>

<div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded p-4">
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Key Takeaway</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">The final net figure = (all emission reductions) + (net carbon removals) − (leakage). Reductions and removals are tracked separately because removals (SOC gains) are reversible and require a buffer deduction, while reductions (avoided GHG emissions) are permanent. Module 4 covers how uncertainty deductions and the buffer pool reduce this number to the final VCU count.</p>
</div>
