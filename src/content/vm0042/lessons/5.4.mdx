{/* 5.4: Navigating the VM0042 Appendices */}

<div class="space-y-6">
  <HighlightBox>
    <h3 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">Your Reference Toolkit</h3>
    <p>VM0042 has 6 appendices that provide the detailed technical procedures, tables, and equations needed to implement the methodology. Think of the main body as the "what and why" and the appendices as the "how." You'll refer to them constantly during project design and monitoring.</p>
  </HighlightBox>
  <AnalogyBox>
    <h4 class="font-semibold mb-1">Analogy: Recipe Book Appendices</h4>
    <p>The main methodology is the recipe. The appendices are the conversion tables, ingredient substitution guide, and equipment procedures. You don't read them start-to-finish — you jump to the right appendix when you need it.</p>
  </AnalogyBox>
  <div class="bg-red-50 border border-red-300 rounded-lg p-4 mb-4">
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-red-800 mb-1">Important Correction</h4>
    <p class="text-sm text-red-700">The VM0042 v2.2 appendices are <strong>not</strong> where you find definitions, applicability conditions, or emission factor tables — those are in the main document (Sections 3, 4, and equation tables). The 6 appendices serve specific supplementary purposes as described below. Definitions are in <strong>Section 3</strong>; applicability conditions are in <strong>Section 4</strong>.</p>
  </div>
  <ExampleBox>
    <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📍 How Appendices Are Used in Practice</p>
    <p class="mt-2 text-sm">A project developer in Maharashtra, India is designing a VM0042 project for 2,000 ha of degraded pastureland that was conventionally grazed for 15 years before being converted to dryland soybean in Year -2. She needs to know: (1) is this land type eligible? (Appendix 2 — degradation procedure), (2) which practices qualify? (Appendix 1 — practice eligibility), and (3) how should she justify her baseline scenario? (Appendix 3). She does not need Appendices 4, 5, or 6 for the design phase. Understanding which appendix handles which question saves hours of searching through the 80+ page methodology document.</p>
  </ExampleBox>

  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Appendix 1: Non-Exhaustive List of Potential Improved ALM Practices</h3>
  <div class="bg-blue-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-blue-200 mb-3">
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-blue-800">When to use it:</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mt-1">During project design to verify that your proposed practice changes are recognized as eligible improved ALM practices, and to get guidance on how practice change is determined for each category.</p>
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-blue-800 mt-3">What it covers:</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mt-1">
      <li>• Non-exhaustive lists of eligible practices for each of the 5 ALM categories (a–e)</li>
      <li>• Guidance on determining what constitutes a "practice change" for each category</li>
      <li>• Notes on quantitative thresholds (e.g., adjustments exceeding 5% of pre-existing practice)</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="text-sm mt-2 italic">Example: Appendix 1 clarifies that a fertilizer rate reduction qualifies only if it exceeds 5% of the historical average rate — helping you confirm your project's eligibility.</p>
  </div>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Appendix 2: Procedure to Demonstrate Degradation of Project Lands</h3>
  <div class="bg-indigo-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-indigo-200 mb-3">
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-indigo-800">When to use it:</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mt-1">Only for projects involving a one-time conversion between cropland and grassland (or vice versa). This is a special case requiring degradation evidence before project validation.</p>
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-indigo-800 mt-3">What it covers:</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mt-1">
      <li>• Step-by-step procedure to document that project lands are degraded at project start</li>
      <li>• Evidence requirements (satellite imagery, soil tests, field surveys)</li>
      <li>• Criteria for demonstrating degradation will continue in the baseline scenario</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="text-sm mt-2 italic">Most ALM projects (continuous cropland or grassland) never need Appendix 2. It only applies under Applicability Condition 3b — when land use type changes within the project.</p>
  </div>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Appendix 3: Selection and Justification of the Baseline Scenario</h3>
  <div class="bg-violet-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-violet-200 mb-3">
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-violet-800">When to use it:</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mt-1">When establishing and documenting the baseline scenario in the Project Description Document (PDD). This provides the justification framework for why "continuation of pre-project practices" is the most plausible baseline.</p>
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-violet-800 mt-3">What it covers:</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mt-1">
      <li>• Rationale for selecting continuation of pre-project ALM practices as baseline</li>
      <li>• Evidence requirements for the baseline scenario selection</li>
      <li>• How to justify the baseline against market trends, policies, and regional practice data</li>
      <li>• Guidance on baseline reassessment triggers and documentation</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Appendix 4: Guidance on Potential Emerging Technologies to Measure SOC Content</h3>
  <div class="bg-green-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-green-200 mb-3">
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-green-800">When to use it:</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mt-1">When evaluating whether to use newer, lower-cost SOC measurement technologies (spectroscopy, remote sensing, etc.) in your monitoring plan instead of, or alongside, traditional wet chemistry.</p>
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-green-800 mt-3">What it covers:</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mt-1">
      <li>• Overview of emerging technologies applicable to SOC measurement</li>
      <li>• Conditions under which each technology may be used within VM0042</li>
      <li>• Validation requirements before a new technology can substitute for standard methods</li>
      <li>• Links to the VT0014 module pathway for new MRV approaches</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="text-sm mt-2 italic">This is the appendix from Lesson 5.3 — the "emerging technologies" content in VM0042 v2.2 lives here, not in a measurement procedures appendix.</p>
  </div>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Appendix 5: Definitions of Soil Slope Classes for Use in Setting Baseline Control Sites</h3>
  <div class="bg-yellow-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-yellow-200 mb-3">
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-yellow-800">When to use it:</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mt-1">When selecting and justifying baseline control sites. Slope class is one of the criteria for ensuring control sites are representative of the project area.</p>
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-yellow-800 mt-3">What it covers:</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mt-1">
      <li>• Standard slope class definitions (e.g., flat 0–2%, gentle 2–5%, moderate 5–15%, steep 15%+)</li>
      <li>• How slope affects SOC dynamics and why matching slope class between project and control sites matters</li>
      <li>• Criteria for slope matching when selecting baseline control sites in Approach 2</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="text-sm mt-2 italic">You only need this appendix if you're using Approach 2 and need to confirm that your baseline control sites match the slope characteristics of your project fields.</p>
  </div>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Appendix 6: Additional Uncertainty Examples</h3>
  <div class="bg-red-50 p-4 rounded-lg border border-red-200 mb-3">
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-red-800">When to use it:</h4>
    <p class="text-sm mt-1">When calculating uncertainty for complex project structures — especially nested/hierarchical designs with multiple quantification unit tiers, or projects combining multiple approaches.</p>
    <h4 class="font-semibold text-red-800 mt-3">What it covers:</h4>
    <ul class="text-sm space-y-1 mt-1">
      <li>• Worked examples of uncertainty propagation for nested quantification unit designs</li>
      <li>• Examples showing how to combine uncertainty from multiple sources (sampling, model, measurement)</li>
      <li>• Guidance on staged/hierarchical designs where primary and secondary QUs are used</li>
    </ul>
    <p class="text-sm mt-2 italic">The main uncertainty equations are in Section 8.6. Appendix 6 provides additional worked examples to illustrate how those equations apply in complex project structures.</p>
