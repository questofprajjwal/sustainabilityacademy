{/* 5.2: The Verification Process */}

<div class="space-y-6">
  <HighlightBox>
    <h3 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">Third-Party Trust</h3>
    <p>Carbon markets work on trust. Buyers pay for VCUs because an independent, accredited auditor has confirmed that emission reductions are real, measurable, and additional. Understanding verification helps project developers prepare correctly and avoid common pitfalls that delay credit issuance.</p>
  </HighlightBox>
  <AnalogyBox>
    <h4 class="font-semibold mb-1">Analogy: Financial Audit</h4>
    <p>Verification is like a company's annual financial audit. The company keeps its own books (monitoring data), but an independent CPA firm reviews them against source documents. In carbon, the project proponent keeps monitoring records, the VVB audits them, and Verra registers the result.</p>
  </AnalogyBox>
  <ExampleBox>
    <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📍 Real Verification: Kenya KACP Project</p>
    <p class="mt-2 text-sm">The Kenya Agricultural Carbon Project (KACP), one of the most cited agricultural soil carbon projects in sub-Saharan Africa, completed its first verification cycle using Bureau Veritas as the VVB. The verification team spent two weeks in the field, visiting 60 of the 60,000 enrolled farmer plots. They checked GPS-recorded field boundaries, asked farmers to demonstrate their understanding of conservation agriculture practices, and cross-checked activity records against satellite imagery (Sentinel-2 bare soil index). Corrective action requests were issued for 4 farmers whose boundary coordinates had shifted, requiring GPS re-measurement before VCUs for those plots could be issued.</p>
  </ExampleBox>

  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Validation vs. Verification, A Critical Distinction</h3>
  <div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-4 mb-4">
    <div class="bg-indigo-50 border border-indigo-200 rounded p-4">
      <h4 class="font-bold text-indigo-800 text-base mb-2">✅ Validation <span class="text-sm font-normal">(happens BEFORE the project starts)</span></h4>
      <p class="text-sm text-indigo-900 mb-2">A VVB reviews the <strong>Project Description Document (PDD)</strong> to confirm that the project design is credible, the methodology is correctly applied, the additionality case is sound, and the monitoring plan is robust.</p>
      <p class="text-sm text-indigo-800"><strong>Output:</strong> Validation Report, project is registered in the Verra registry and can begin.</p>
      <p class="text-sm text-indigo-800 mt-1"><strong>Timing:</strong> Once, at project start (before crediting period begins).</p>
      <p class="text-sm text-indigo-700 mt-1"><em>Think of it as: "Is this a credible plan?"</em></p>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-teal-50 border border-teal-200 rounded p-4">
      <h4 class="font-bold text-teal-800 text-base mb-2">📋 Verification <span class="text-sm font-normal">(happens AFTER each monitoring period)</span></h4>
      <p class="text-sm text-teal-900 mb-2">A VVB reviews the <strong>Monitoring Report</strong> for a completed monitoring period to confirm that actual field data supports the claimed GHG reductions. The VVB audits the numbers, visits the site, and certifies the net ERR.</p>
      <p class="text-sm text-teal-800"><strong>Output:</strong> Verification Report + VCU issuance by Verra.</p>
      <p class="text-sm text-teal-800 mt-1"><strong>Timing:</strong> Periodically (often every 5 years); can overlap with the next monitoring period.</p>
      <p class="text-sm text-teal-700 mt-1"><em>Think of it as: "Did the plan actually deliver?"</em></p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-gray-50 border rounded p-3 mb-4 text-sm">
    <p class="font-semibold text-gray-700">Can the same VVB do both?</p>
    <p class="mt-1 text-gray-600">VCS rules require <strong>independence</strong>: the VVB that validates the project cannot verify the first monitoring period for that same project. This two-body rule prevents conflicts of interest (the same auditor shouldn't approve their own prior work). After the first verification, the same or a different VVB may do subsequent verifications.</p>
  </div>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">The 5-Step Verification Process</h3>
  <div class="space-y-3">
    <div class="bg-blue-50 border-l-4 border-blue-500 p-4 rounded">
      <h4 class="font-bold text-blue-800">Step 1: Contract and Scoping</h4>
      <p class="text-sm mt-1">Project developer contracts an accredited VVB. The VVB reviews project documents to understand scope, methodology version, and key risks. A verification plan is agreed upon.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-indigo-50 border-l-4 border-indigo-500 p-4 rounded">
      <h4 class="font-bold text-indigo-800">Step 2: Desktop Review</h4>
      <p class="text-sm mt-1">VVB reviews all submitted documents: Monitoring Report, data files, sampling records, lab reports, activity logs, and model outputs. Clarification requests (CRs) and corrective action requests (CARs) are issued for gaps.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-violet-50 border-l-4 border-violet-500 p-4 rounded">
      <h4 class="font-bold text-violet-800">Step 3: Site Visit</h4>
      <p class="text-sm mt-1">VVB auditors visit project fields to confirm boundaries, interview farmers about practices, observe monitoring procedures, and cross-check activity data against farm records.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-purple-50 border-l-4 border-purple-500 p-4 rounded">
      <h4 class="font-bold text-purple-800">Step 4: Verification Report and Findings</h4>
      <p class="text-sm mt-1">VVB issues a Verification Report stating verified net ERRs (tCO2e), any deductions applied, and conclusion. Unresolved CARs must be addressed before credits can be issued.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-pink-50 border-l-4 border-pink-500 p-4 rounded">
      <h4 class="font-bold text-pink-800">Step 5: Verra Registration and VCU Issuance</h4>
      <p class="text-sm mt-1">Verra reviews the Verification Report and project documentation. If satisfied, Verra issues VCUs to the project's registry account. VCUs are now tradeable on the voluntary carbon market.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>Mock Verification Scenario:</strong><br/>
    A VVB auditor arrives at a rice paddy project in Vietnam. She asks to see activity records for 30 randomly selected farmers. The field officer retrieves mobile app logs, but 6 farmers' records are missing because they switched phones. The auditor issues a CAR: "Provide or reconstruct records for 6 missing farmers, or exclude those fields." The developer reconstructs 4 from paper receipts and excludes 2 fields. The VVB re-reviews and closes the CAR. VCUs are issued for the reduced area.
  </ExampleBox>
  <HighlightBox>
    <h4 class="font-semibold mb-2">Key Takeaways, Lesson 5.2</h4>
    <ul class="space-y-1">
      <li>✓ <strong>Validation</strong> (before project start) confirms the plan is credible; <strong>verification</strong> (after monitoring) confirms it delivered</li>
      <li>✓ VCS requires a different VVB for validation vs. first verification (independence rule)</li>
      <li>✓ Verification is a 5-step process: contract, desktop review, site visit, report, registration</li>
      <li>✓ The VVB is independent, they confirm data, not collect it</li>
      <li>✓ CARs must be resolved before VCUs are issued</li>
      <li>✓ Prevention is cheaper than correction, good record-keeping from day 1 saves money</li>
    </ul>
