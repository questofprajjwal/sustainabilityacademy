<h2 class="text-2xl font-bold text-gray-800 mb-4">From Net Reductions to Verified Carbon Units (VCUs)</h2>
<HighlightBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">The final calculation</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">This lesson completes the journey from raw SOC measurements and emission calculations to the final number of tradeable carbon credits. Three adjustments transform your gross estimate into VCUs: uncertainty deduction, separation of removals and reductions, and buffer pool contribution.</p>
</HighlightBox>

<ExampleBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📍 Buffer Pool in Action, A Brazilian Project</p>
  <p class="mt-2 text-sm">A 15,000 ha integrated crop-livestock project in Mato Grosso, Brazil received a non-permanence risk rating of 22% (buffer contribution). In Year 5, a severe La Niña drought reduced soil moisture dramatically, and satellite NDVI data showed significant vegetation stress. The developer filed an unintentional reversal report within 30 days. Verra placed 18,000 buffer credits on hold. After a VVB field verification confirmed carbon losses of 12,000 tCO₂e, those buffer credits were cancelled from the pooled account. The project's tradeable VCUs were not affected, buyers who had already retired credits were fully protected by the buffer pool's insurance function.</p>
</ExampleBox>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">The VCU Calculation Pathway</h3>
<div class="bg-gray-50 border rounded p-5 mb-6">
  <div class="space-y-3 text-sm">
    <div class="flex items-center gap-3">
      <span class="bg-green-700 text-white rounded-full w-7 h-7 flex items-center justify-center font-bold flex-shrink-0">1</span>
      <div><strong>Start with:</strong> Gross ERR (Estimated Reductions and Removals) from Module 3<br /><span class="text-gray-500">= All emission reductions + All carbon removals − Leakage</span></div>
    </div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-3">
      <span class="bg-green-700 text-white rounded-full w-7 h-7 flex items-center justify-center font-bold flex-shrink-0">2</span>
      <div><strong>Apply:</strong> Uncertainty deduction<br /><span class="text-gray-500">= Gross ERR × (1 − UNC) per gas/pool</span></div>
    </div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-3">
      <span class="bg-green-700 text-white rounded-full w-7 h-7 flex items-center justify-center font-bold flex-shrink-0">3</span>
      <div><strong>Separate:</strong> Removals (SOC gains) vs Reductions (avoided GHG emissions)<br /><span class="text-gray-500">Important: buffer only applies to removals, not reductions</span></div>
    </div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-3">
      <span class="bg-green-700 text-white rounded-full w-7 h-7 flex items-center justify-center font-bold flex-shrink-0">4</span>
      <div><strong>Apply buffer pool:</strong> Removals only<br /><span class="text-gray-500">= Removals × buffer % deposited to Verra's AFOLU pooled buffer account</span></div>
    </div>
    <div class="flex items-center gap-3">
      <span class="bg-amber-600 text-white rounded-full w-7 h-7 flex items-center justify-center font-bold flex-shrink-0">5</span>
      <div><strong>Result:</strong> VCUs issued = (Removals × (1 − buffer%)) + Reductions</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

<h3 class="text-xl font-bold mt-6 mb-3">The Buffer Pool, Permanence Risk Insurance</h3>
<AnalogyBox>
  <p class="font-semibold text-blue-800">🏠 Analogy: Home Insurance</p>
  <p class="mt-1 text-sm text-blue-900">If you own a house, you pay insurance premiums (a % of its value) so that if something goes wrong, the pool can cover losses. Similarly, projects contribute VCUs to the buffer so that if one project reverses (farmer reverts to tillage, releasing stored carbon), the market's integrity is maintained from the pooled buffer.</p>
</AnalogyBox>
<p class="text-sm mb-3">Buffer % is determined by Verra's <strong>AFOLU Non-Permanence Risk Tool</strong>, it assesses the project's risk of reversal based on:</p>
<ul class="list-disc ml-6 text-sm space-y-1 mb-4">
  <li>Socio-economic risks (farmer/landowner stability, governance)</li>
  <li>Natural risks (drought, flooding, pests)</li>
  <li>Internal project risks (management capacity, monitoring quality)</li>
</ul>
<p class="text-sm mb-4">Typical buffer percentages: <strong>10–20%</strong> for most agricultural soil carbon projects.</p>

<div class="bg-orange-50 border border-orange-300 rounded-lg p-4 mb-4">
  <h4 class="font-bold text-orange-800 mb-2">Critical Distinction: Intentional vs. Unintentional Reversals</h4>
  <p class="text-sm text-orange-900 mb-2">Not all reversals are treated equally in VM0042 and the VCS Standard. Understanding the difference is critical for developers and investors:</p>
  <div class="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-2 gap-3 text-sm">
    <div class="bg-yellow-100 p-3 rounded border border-yellow-300">
      <h5 class="font-bold text-yellow-800 mb-1">Unintentional Reversals</h5>
      <p class="text-yellow-900">Caused by unavoidable events outside the developer's control, natural disasters (drought, flood, wildfire), extreme weather, disease outbreaks. The <strong>buffer pool covers these losses</strong>. The project does not owe additional credits; the buffer absorbs the reversal.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="bg-red-100 p-3 rounded border border-red-300">
      <h5 class="font-bold text-red-800 mb-1">Intentional Reversals</h5>
      <p class="text-red-900">Caused by deliberate decisions, a farmer knowingly plows a no-till field, exits the contract, or the project developer discontinues the project. The buffer pool does NOT cover these. The project proponent is <strong>contractually required to replace the cancelled credits</strong>, either by retiring equivalent VCUs from their own account or sourcing equivalent credits from the market. This is a major financial liability.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <p class="text-sm mt-3 text-orange-800"><strong>Practical implication:</strong> VM0042 projects with thousands of smallholder farmers must include binding agreements with each farmer (contract, participation agreement, or community covenant) specifying consequences for intentional reversal. Without this, the project developer bears the full financial risk for every farmer who exits the programme.</p>
</div>
<div class="bg-green-50 border border-green-200 rounded p-4">
  <p class="font-semibold text-green-800">Key Takeaway</p>
  <p class="text-green-900 mt-1">VCUs = (Net removals × (1 − buffer%)) + Net reductions. The buffer pool covers unintentional reversals (natural events). Intentional reversals must be made whole by the project proponent, a significant contractual and financial liability that must be addressed in farmer agreements from the start.</p>
</div>
