  <HighlightBox>
    <h3 class="font-bold text-lg mb-2">The Monitoring Imperative</h3>
    <p>Carbon credits are only as credible as the data behind them. VM0042's monitoring requirements ensure that every VCU issued reflects a real, measured reduction in greenhouse gases — not a promise. Section 9 of VM0042 is the operational backbone of any project.</p>
  </HighlightBox>
  <AnalogyBox>
    <h4 class="font-semibold mb-1">Analogy: Health Monitoring</h4>
    <p>Think of monitoring like a patient's health tracking program. You don't just weigh the patient once at the start — you regularly check weight, blood pressure, and lab results to confirm the treatment is working. VM0042 monitoring works the same way: regular sampling confirms soil carbon accumulation is real and ongoing.</p>
  </AnalogyBox>
  <ExampleBox>
    <p class="font-semibold text-amber-800">📍 What Monitoring Failure Looks Like</p>
    <p class="mt-2 text-sm">In a 2021 VVB audit of a rice AWD project in Myanmar, field officers had been recording nitrogen fertilizer application rates from memory rather than actual bag counts. When the VVB cross-checked the farm records against supplier invoices (available from a local agri-dealer), actual fertilizer use was found to be 15% higher than reported — significantly reducing the N₂O reduction credit. The lesson: monitoring is only valuable if it captures what actually happened, not what was planned.</p>
  </ExampleBox>

  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">The QU System: Quantification Units</h3>
  <p>A <strong>quantification unit (QU)</strong> is the unit of land for which a discrete amount of ERRs is quantified. This could be a single farmer's field, a cluster of fields, or a stratum. Monitoring is designed at the QU level.</p>
  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>Why it matters:</strong> A project with 5,000 farmers cannot sample every field. Instead it samples a statistically representative subset of QUs within each stratum to estimate the whole. Sampling design determines accuracy and therefore uncertainty deductions.
  </ExampleBox>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Sampling Design Principles</h3>
  <p>VM0042 requires SOC sampling achieve a 90% confidence interval within ±10% of the mean SOC stock change. To achieve this:</p>
  <ul class="list-disc pl-6 space-y-2">
    <li><strong>Minimum plots per stratum:</strong> Statistical formulas based on pilot variance determine plot count</li>
    <li><strong>Stratified random sampling:</strong> Fields divided into strata; random plots chosen within each</li>
    <li><strong>Permanent vs. temporary plots:</strong> Permanent plots track change at the same location (preferred for Approach 2)</li>
    <li><strong>Composite samples:</strong> Multiple cores per plot composited to reduce lab cost</li>
  </ul>
  <h3 class="text-xl font-bold text-gray-800">Real Example: Zambia Cooperative Monitoring Plan</h3>
  <ExampleBox>
    <strong>Scenario:</strong> 3,200 smallholders in Zambia switching to conservation agriculture. Project uses Approach 2 (Measure and Re-Measure).<br/><br/>
    <strong>Monitoring Plan Summary:</strong>
    <ul class="list-disc pl-5 mt-2 space-y-1">
      <li>2 strata: Sandy soils (1,800 ha) and Clay-loam (1,400 ha)</li>
      <li>Pilot CV: 35% sandy, 28% clay-loam</li>
      <li>Required plots: 47 permanent in sandy stratum, 31 in clay-loam = 78 total</li>
      <li>Sampling depth: 0-30 cm in 3 increments</li>
      <li>Frequency: Every 5 years; activity data annually via mobile app</li>
      <li>QA/QC: 10% re-sampling by independent technician; accredited lab required</li>
    </ul>
  </ExampleBox>

        <li>• ISO 17025 accredited laboratory</li>
        <li>• Certified reference standards each batch</li>
        <li>• Blind duplicate analysis (5% of samples)</li>
        <li>• Results reviewed for outliers</li>
      </ul>

    <h4 class="font-semibold mb-2">Key Takeaways — Lesson 5.1</h4>
    <ul class="space-y-1">
      <li>✓ Monitoring covers activity data (what farmers did) and SOC stocks (what soil stores)</li>
      <li>✓ Sampling targets ±10% precision at 90% confidence</li>
      <li>✓ QA/QC is mandatory — field, lab, and data management protocols all required</li>
      <li>✓ Monitoring events and verification events are distinct but linked</li>
