## What Is Step D?

Step D is the reporting step. Once the materiality assessment is complete, the undertaking must report on both the process it followed and the results it reached.

EFRAG IG1 paragraph 99 explains that following the materiality assessment process, the undertaking shall report on the assessment process and its outcome based on three specific disclosure requirements.

## Three Mandatory Disclosures

### ESRS 2 IRO-1: The Process

The first required disclosure is ESRS 2 IRO-1, which requires a description of the processes used to identify and assess material impacts, risks, and opportunities. This covers how the materiality assessment was designed and conducted. It includes the methodologies and assumptions applied, the focus and extent of the process, and the inputs used.

EFRAG IG1 paragraph 29 lists what this disclosure includes: "methodologies and assumptions applied, the focus and extent of the process, and inputs." ESRS 2 IRO-1 also requires transparency on the judgement exercised, including quantitative or qualitative thresholds and other criteria used.

### ESRS 2 SBM-3: The Outcome and Its Connection to Strategy

The second required disclosure is ESRS 2 SBM-3, which covers the material impacts, risks, and opportunities and their interaction with the undertaking's strategy and business model. This disclosure connects the output of the materiality assessment to the broader context of how the undertaking operates and where it is heading.

ESRS 1 paragraph 26 states that "SBM-3 of ESRS 2 provides general disclosure requirements on the material impacts, risks and opportunities resulting from the undertaking's materiality assessment."

### ESRS 2 IRO-2: Which ESRS Are Covered

The third required disclosure is ESRS 2 IRO-2, titled "Disclosure requirements in ESRS covered by the undertaking's sustainability statement." The undertaking must also disclose how it has determined the material information to be disclosed, including the thresholds and criteria used to assess such information (ESRS 2 paragraph 59, referenced in EFRAG IG1 paragraph 99).

This disclosure makes clear which topical ESRS standards the undertaking's sustainability statement actually covers, based on the outcome of the materiality assessment.

<ExampleBox>
A mid-size manufacturing company completes its materiality assessment and reaches the following reporting outcome at a high level:

**IRO-1 disclosure (the process):** The company describes how it used the ESRS 1 AR16 list as a starting point, reviewed the results of its existing risk management process and due diligence programme, engaged affected stakeholder groups in three workshops, and applied quantitative magnitude thresholds of 2% of revenue for financial effects and a five-level severity scale for impacts.

**SBM-3 disclosure (the outcome linked to strategy):** The company states that it identified 14 material IROs, including actual negative impacts on worker health and safety in its own operations and upstream supply chain, financial risks from physical climate hazards to its main production facility in a flood-prone coastal area, and opportunities from growing demand for its lower-carbon product lines. It describes how these material IROs interact with its current business model and long-term strategy.

**IRO-2 disclosure (the ESRS covered):** The company presents a table showing which topical ESRS it has concluded are covered by its sustainability statement: ESRS E1 (climate change), ESRS S1 (own workforce), and ESRS S2 (workers in the value chain). It includes an explanation of why ESRS E3 (water) was assessed as not material for climate change, along with the forward-looking analysis required for that conclusion.
</ExampleBox>

## Comparative Information

ESRS 1 section 7.1 requires the undertaking to disclose comparative information in respect of the previous period for all quantitative metrics and monetary amounts disclosed in the current period. When relevant to understanding the current period's sustainability statement, the undertaking shall also disclose comparative information for narrative disclosures (ESRS 1 paragraph 83).

When the undertaking reports comparative information that differs from the information reported in the previous period, it must disclose both the difference and the reasons for the revision (ESRS 1 paragraph 84).

When it is not practicable to adjust comparative information for prior periods, the undertaking must disclose this fact (ESRS 1 paragraph 85).

## The Structure of the Sustainability Statement

ESRS 1 paragraph 115 requires the undertaking to structure its sustainability statement in four parts, in the following order:

1. **General information**
2. **Environmental information** (including disclosures pursuant to Article 8 of the Taxonomy Regulation)
3. **Social information**
4. **Governance information**

This four-part structure applies to the full sustainability statement. Respecting the provision in ESRS 1 section 3.6 on material impacts or risks arising from actions to address sustainability matters, when information provided in one part contains information to be reported in another part, the undertaking may refer in one part to information presented in another part, avoiding duplications (ESRS 1 paragraph 115).

The disclosures required by sector-specific ESRS are grouped by reporting area and, where applicable, by sustainability topic, alongside the disclosures required by ESRS 2 and the corresponding topical ESRS (ESRS 1 paragraph 116).

## What "Omissions" Mean in Practice

### Explicit Omissions: EU-Legislation-Derived Datapoints

When the undertaking omits a datapoint that derives from other EU legislation listed in ESRS 2 Appendix B because it is not considered material, it must explicitly state that the information is "not material" (ESRS 1 paragraph 35). EFRAG IG1 paragraph 60 confirms this: "the undertaking is not considered material, the undertaking has to include an explicit statement saying that such a datapoint is 'not material'."

ESRS 2 also requires the undertaking to include in the sustainability statement a table of all such datapoints, specifying where they can be found in the statement, and for those that are omitted, reporting that the respective datapoint is not material (EFRAG IG1 paragraph 60).

### Implicit Omissions: Other Datapoints

For Disclosure Requirements and datapoints not derived from EU legislation, the undertaking may simply omit them if they are assessed to not be material and are not needed to meet the objective of the Disclosure Requirement (ESRS 1 paragraph 34(b)). There is no requirement to explicitly explain the omission in these cases, though the undertaking may briefly explain the conclusions of its materiality assessment for any omitted topic (EFRAG IG1 paragraph 31).

There is one important exception. If the undertaking concludes that climate change is not material and therefore omits all disclosure requirements in ESRS E1, it must disclose a detailed explanation of the conclusions of its materiality assessment with regard to climate change, including a forward-looking analysis of the conditions that could lead the undertaking to conclude that climate change is material in the future (ESRS 1 paragraph 32).

## Updating the Materiality Assessment

ESRS 1 paragraph 95 states that the definition and calculation of metrics shall be consistent over time. If the undertaking redefines or replaces a metric or target, or identifies new information that was relevant in a prior period, it shall provide restated comparative figures, unless it is not practicable to do so.

The materiality assessment itself is not a one-time exercise. As the undertaking's business model, strategy, or operating context evolves, its materiality conclusions may change. When this occurs, the undertaking updates its assessment and reflects the changes in its reporting, with appropriate comparative information.

<DeepDive title="What must always be disclosed regardless of the materiality outcome?">
Irrespective of the outcome of its materiality assessment, the undertaking shall always disclose the information required by ESRS 2 General Disclosures, meaning all the Disclosure Requirements and data points specified in ESRS 2, and the Disclosure Requirement IRO-1 as listed in ESRS 2 Appendix C (ESRS 1 paragraph 29).

This means that even if the undertaking concludes that a given sustainability topic is not material, the cross-cutting disclosures in ESRS 2 about governance, strategy, and the materiality process itself must still be provided.
</DeepDive>
